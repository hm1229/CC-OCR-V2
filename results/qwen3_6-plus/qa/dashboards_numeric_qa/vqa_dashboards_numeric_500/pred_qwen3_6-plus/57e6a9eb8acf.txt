To calculate the **percentage** of senior citizens (aged 65 and above) in Singapore’s citizen population in **1998**, we use the data provided:

> “1.4 in 20 citizens are aged 65 years and above”

This means:

\[
\frac{1.4}{20} = 0.07
\]

Convert to percentage:

\[
0.07 \times 100 = 7\%
\]

---

✅ **Answer: 7%**

*(Note: The infographic uses “1.4 in 20” — which is equivalent to 7% — to represent the proportion of seniors in 1998.)*
