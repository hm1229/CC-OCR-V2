The developmental benefits of early childhood education include:  

- **Social and emotional**: Students develop relationships with adults and peers, gain a sense of security from their constant presence, and experience greater participation and cooperation due to this sense of security.  
- **Cognitive**: A developmentally appropriate curriculum allows age - appropriate learning. Students build on existing skills to increase their knowledge, and teachers support students in reaching important developmental milestones.  
- **Language**: Between ages 1 and 5, language develops quickly. Routine interactions with teachers and peers develop language skills, and more advanced skills emerge as conversations and vocabulary programs progress.
