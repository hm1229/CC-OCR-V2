The **Louisiana Purchase** is regarded as one of the greatest real estate deals in U.S. history.

In 1803, President Thomas Jefferson led the acquisition of the Louisiana Territory from France for **$15 million** — approximately **3 cents per acre**. This purchase doubled the size of the United States overnight, adding about **828,000 square miles** of land stretching from the Mississippi River to the Rocky Mountains and from the Gulf of Mexico to the Canadian border.

Today, that same $15 million would be worth roughly **$284 million**, yet the land now comprises parts of **15 different states** and remains a cornerstone of American expansion and development.
