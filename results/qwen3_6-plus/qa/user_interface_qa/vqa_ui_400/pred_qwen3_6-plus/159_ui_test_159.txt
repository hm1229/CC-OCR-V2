Based on the infographic, the **three inventions introduced at the 1904 St. Louis World’s Fair** are:

1. **The Ice Cream Cone**  
   – Debuted as the first edible, walk-away ice cream container. Its popularity led to the development of industrial cone-rolling machines.

2. **Electricity (for the masses)**  
   – Celebrated with “The Palace of Electricity,” overseen by Thomas Edison. This marked a major public showcase of electrical technology for everyday use.

3. **The Hot Dog**  
   – Born when sausage seller Anton Feuchtwanger ran out of gloves and asked his brother-in-law to create a sausage-sized bun — leading to the iconic hot dog in a bun.

---

✅ So, the three inventions from the 1904 St. Louis World’s Fair listed in the infographic are:  
> **Ice Cream Cone**, **Electricity (public presentation)**, and **Hot Dog**

*(Note: While electricity itself wasn’t “invented” in 1904, its large-scale public demonstration and promotion for mass use was a key feature of that Expo — hence its inclusion as an “invention” in this context.)*
