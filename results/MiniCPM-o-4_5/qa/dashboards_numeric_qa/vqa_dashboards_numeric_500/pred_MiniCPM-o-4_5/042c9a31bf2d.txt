Looking at the "LONG-TERM BENEFITS" section of the infographic, there are two main categories: "Student success" and "Adult responsibility".

Under "Student success", it states:
- Students with early childhood education are 29% more likely to graduate from high school.
- Students with early childhood education are 44% less likely to repeat a grade.
- Students with early childhood education pass standardized tests more frequently.

Under "Adult responsibility", it states:
- Adults age 40 who had early childhood education are more likely to be employed.
- These 40-year-olds also have a 33% higher average income.
- 18-year-olds without early childhood education are 70% more likely to be arrested for a violent crime.

Therefore, the long-term benefits include improved academic outcomes (graduation, test performance, grade repetition), better employment and income prospects in adulthood, and reduced likelihood of criminal behavior.

Answer: Long-term benefits include higher high school graduation rates, less likelihood of repeating a grade, better performance on standardized tests, greater likelihood of employment and higher average income by age 40, and lower likelihood of being arrested for violent crime.
