This document is a **Discipline Review Letter** issued by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to Takeda Global Research & Development Center, Inc.

Here is a breakdown of what that means:

*   **Purpose:** A Discipline Review Letter is a formal communication sent by the FDA to a drug sponsor (in this case, Takeda) during the review process of a New Drug Application (NDA). Its purpose is to inform the company of the FDA's preliminary findings or concerns regarding specific sections of their application before a final decision is made.
*   **Context:** This specific letter concerns **Actos (pioglitazone HCl)**. The FDA is explaining why they are rejecting Takeda’s proposal to include certain data from the "PROactive trial" in the "Clinical Studies" section of the drug's official labeling.
*   **Key Content:** The FDA is telling Takeda that they cannot move the PROactive trial data to the "Clinical Studies" section because they consider the trial to have "failed." The FDA argues that because the trial did not meet its primary endpoint, the secondary endpoints cannot be considered statistically valid and appear to be a "post hoc analysis" (an analysis performed after the data was already known) rather than a prospectively planned study.

In short, the FDA is acting as a regulator, challenging the company's attempt to present the results of a clinical trial in a way that the FDA believes is scientifically misleading or statistically invalid.
