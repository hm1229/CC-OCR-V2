According to the infographic:

> **A 60-watt bulb can use up to 5 gallons of water for every hour it’s left on.**

This refers to the “hidden water” — the water used indirectly in generating the electricity that powers the bulb (primarily through thermoelectric power generation, which accounts for 49% of American water use).

---

✅ So, the answer is:  
**5 gallons per hour**

*(Note: This is not water consumed by the bulb itself, but rather the water used in the power plant to generate the electricity needed to run the bulb for one hour.)*
