Based on the infographic, **2 in 5 people with disability are 65 years or older**.

This means that among Australians living with a disability, **40% (or 2/5)** are aged 65 or over.

Additionally, the infographic highlights that:

> “The likelihood of living with disability increases with age.”

And it provides specific statistics for age groups:

- **1 in 8 (12.5%)** of Australians **under 65** have a disability.
- **50.7%** of Australians **aged 65+** have a disability — which is nearly half of that age group.

---

### So, to directly answer your question:

> **What is the chance of disability for senior citizens?**

✅ **Approximately 50.7%** — meaning more than half of Australians aged 65 and over live with some form of disability.

This is significantly higher than the national average of 1 in 5 (20%) and much higher than the 12.5% rate for those under 65.
