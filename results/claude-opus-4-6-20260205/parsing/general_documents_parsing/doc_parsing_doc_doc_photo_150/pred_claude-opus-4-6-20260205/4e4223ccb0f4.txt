\textbf{1.} \quad Express each number as a product of its prime factors:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] 140

    Prime factors of $140 = 2, 2, 5, 7$
    \[= 2^2 \times 5 \times 7\]

    \item[(ii)] 156

    Prime factors of $156 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 13$
    \[= 2^2 \times 3 \times 13\]

    \item[(iii)] 3825

    Prime factors of $3825 = 3 \times 3 \times 5 \times 5 \times 17$
    \[= 3^2 \times 5^2 \times 17\]

    \item[(iv)] 5005

    Prime factors of $5005 = 5 \times 7 \times 11 \times 13.$

    \item[(v)] 7429

    Prime factors of $7429 = 17 \times 19 \times 23$
\end{enumerate}

\bigskip

\textbf{2.} \quad Find the LCM and HCF of the following pairs of integers and verify that LCM $\times$ HCF = product of the two numbers

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] 26 and 91

    Prime factors of $26 = 2 \times 13$

    Prime factors of $91 = 7 \times 13$

    \bigskip

    HCF of 26 and $91 = 13$

    LCM of 26 and $91 = 2 \times 7 \times 13$
    \[= 182\]

    \bigskip

    Product of two numbers $= 26 \times 91 = 2366$

    LCM $\times$ HCF $= 182 \times 13 = 2366$

    \bigskip

    So, Product of two numbers $=$ LCM $\times$ HCF
\end{enumerate}
