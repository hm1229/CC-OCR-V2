The phase of love described as having a primitive impulse is "Limerence." Limerence is characterized by an intense, often obsessive, emotional state driven by an increase in dopamine, which motivates the individual to pursue a reward. This phase can sometimes lead to obsession due to decreased serotonin levels. Additionally, limerence is associated with increased testosterone in men and estrogen in women, which may help men focus their sexual interest. Over time, limerence tends to diminish, while attachment grows stronger in healthy and sustainable relationships.
