The ratio of the U.S. population to bank branches in 1970 was 9,340. This indicates that for every bank branch, there were 9,340 people in the population. This high ratio reflects the era when physical bank branches were the primary means of banking, and access to banking services was largely dependent on the presence of these branches in various locations. Over the years, with advancements in technology and the rise of online banking, this ratio has significantly decreased, as more people can access banking services from the comfort of their homes without needing to visit a physical branch.
