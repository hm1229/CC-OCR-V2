4. Consider the following table:  

\[
\begin{array}{c|ccccc}
x & 0.3 & 0.4 & 0.5 & 0.6 & 0.7 \\
\hline
f(x) & 1.5 & 2 & 5 & 7 & 12 \\
\end{array}
\]  

Let \( x_{i+1} = x_i + h \), \( i = 0, \dots, 3 \).  

(a) Approximate \( f'(x) \) using \( D_h^+ f(0.5) \) and \( h = 0.1 \).  

(b) Compute \( D_h^{(2)} f(0.5) \) using the Central Difference Formula and step size \( h = 0.2 \).  

Note: You may use the following formula for Central Difference Formula:  
\[
D_h^{(2)} f(x_i) = \frac{D_h^+ f(x_i) - D_h f(x_i)}{h}
\]  

(c) Compare the answer from (b) with the following approximation:  
\[
D_h^{(2)} f(x_i) = \frac{f(x_2) - 2f(x_1) + f(x_0)}{h^2},
\]  
with \( x_1 = 0.5 \), \( h = 0.2 \).
