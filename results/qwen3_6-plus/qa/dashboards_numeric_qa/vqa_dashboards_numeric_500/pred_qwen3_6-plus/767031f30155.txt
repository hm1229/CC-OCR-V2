Based on the provided infographic titled **“COVID-19: Canada’s Economic Response Plan”**, here are the **emergency benefits provided to working Canadians**:

---

### 1. **Emergency Care Benefit**
- **Amount**: Up to **$900 bi-weekly** for up to **15 weeks**.
- **Eligibility**: For individuals who must stay home due to COVID-19 and **do not have paid sick leave**.
  - Includes those who:
    - Can’t access Employment Insurance (EI),
    - Are sick,
    - Are taking care of a child or someone who is sick.

---

### 2. **Emergency Support Benefit**
- **Amount**: Up to **$5 billion** in total support (not per person).
- **Eligibility**: For workers who:
  - Are **not eligible for EI**, and
  - Are facing **unemployment or reduced hours**.

> *Note: This benefit was designed as a temporary measure to support those falling through the cracks of existing programs.*

---

### Additional Context from the Infographic:
The plan also includes broader economic supports that indirectly benefit working Canadians:

- **$27 Billion** → Direct support for Canadian workers and businesses.
- **$55 Billion** → Support in business liquidity through tax deferrals (helps employers retain workers).

---

### Summary:
Working Canadians were offered two key emergency benefits during the early phase of the pandemic:

✅ **Emergency Care Benefit** — $900 every 2 weeks for up to 15 weeks, for those unable to work due to caregiving or illness without paid leave.

✅ **Emergency Support Benefit** — A $5 billion fund to support workers ineligible for EI who lost jobs or hours.

These were part of Canada’s broader $82+ billion economic response package aimed at protecting workers and businesses during the crisis.

*(Note: These programs were time-limited and have since been replaced or ended as the pandemic evolved.)*
