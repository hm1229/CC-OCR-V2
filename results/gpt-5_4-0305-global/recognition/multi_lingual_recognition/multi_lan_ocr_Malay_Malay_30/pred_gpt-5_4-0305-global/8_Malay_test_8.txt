Under Budgeting Mechanism

A. T/F

1. Participatory budgeting is a process where all members of a community directly decide how to spend part of a public budget.
2. The main goal of participatory budgeting is to increase transparency and citizen engagement in public spending.
3. In a line-item budget, funds are allocated based on specific categories such as salaries, supplies, and equipment.
4. Program budgeting focuses on the inputs used by government agencies rather than the outcomes of their programs.
5. Zero-based budgeting requires every expense to be justified from scratch for each new budget period.
6. A balanced budget occurs when government revenues exceed expenditures.
7. Deficit budgeting is often used during economic downturns to stimulate growth through increased public spending.
8. Surplus budgeting means the government spends more than it collects in revenue.
9. Performance budgeting links funding to measurable results and outcomes.
10. A key advantage of line-item budgeting is that it makes it easier to evaluate the effectiveness of government programs.
