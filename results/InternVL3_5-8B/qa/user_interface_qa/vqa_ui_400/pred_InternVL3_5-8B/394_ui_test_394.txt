The biscuits used in WWI soldier rations were Anzac biscuits. These biscuits were originally made as a bread substitute for the soldiers, and they were hard and savory. They became closely associated with the Australian and New Zealand Army Corps (ANZAC), established in WWI. The recipe for Anzac biscuits was first published in "The War Chest Cookery Book" in 1917. They are made from oats, coconut, syrup, and sugar, and are often described as having an "acquired taste." During WWII, they were rationed across Australia to meet demand. In 1984, they were the first product to be electronically scanned at a grocery store in Australia. Anzac Day, observed on April 25th, commemorates the landing of the ANZAC troops at Gallipoli in 1915 and is celebrated with these biscuits.
