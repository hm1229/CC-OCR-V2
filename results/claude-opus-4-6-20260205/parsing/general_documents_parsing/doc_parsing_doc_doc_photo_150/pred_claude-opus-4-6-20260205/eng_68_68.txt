For each of the following formulae, choose the sentence that provides the best English translation of the formula according to the following symbolization key:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|l|}
\hline
$B$ & Bob is gathering eggs. \\
\hline
$C$ & Bob has fed the chickens. \\
\hline
$D$ & Bob has fed the ducks. \\
\hline
$F$ & Bob is chasing the fox away. \\
\hline
$P$ & The ducks are playing in the pond. \\
\hline
$Q$ & The ducks are quacking loudly. \\
\hline
$S$ & The chickens are playing in the pond. \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section*{Question 10}

$(F \rightarrow (Q\ \&\ \neg S))$

\hfill Select one answer.

\hfill 2 points

\begin{enumerate}[label=\Alph*.]
\item $\bigcirc$ If the ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond, then Bob is chasing the fox away.
\item $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond unless Bob is chasing the fox away.
\item $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond if Bob is chasing the fox away.
\item $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond only if Bob is chasing the fox away.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Question 11}

$((P\ \&\ Q)\ \&\ S)$

\hfill Select one answer.

\hfill 2 points

\begin{enumerate}[label=\Alph*.]
\item $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and playing in the pond, and the chickens are also playing in the pond.
\end{enumerate}
