According to the infographic **“Reading Like a Sleuth”**, under the section:

> **WHAT CLOSE READERS DO DIFFERENTLY**

It contrasts “Close Readers” with “Not-So-Close Readers.”

Under **“Not-So-Close Readers…”**, it lists:

> **Let their thinking wander.**

---

✅ So, the answer is:  
**Not-so-close readers** let their thinking wander.

This is presented as a key difference — close readers stay focused on the text, while not-so-close readers allow their minds to drift away from it.
