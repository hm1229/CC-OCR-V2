# Phases of Matter in Chemistry

According to the map of chemistry, the different phases of matter are:

1. **Solid** (represented by a crystal/mineral illustration)
2. **Liquid** (represented by water/liquid illustration)
3. **Gas** (represented by a gas cloud illustration)

These three classical phases are shown in the **"PHASES"** section of the map, located in the middle-left area, with arrows indicating transitions between the states (such as the relationship with energy needed to change phases).

The map also shows related concepts nearby, such as:
- **Mixtures** (cake, air) as examples of combined matter
- **Chemical Compounds** (H₂O, CO₂, ammonia) as specific forms of matter
- The note that some substances are **"NOT BURNY OR EXPLODY"** while others are **"BURNY EXPLODY"**
