Based on the infographic "The Science Behind Google," the three basic approaches for organizing websites (which is the context for organizing a network of web pages) are:

1.  **Crawling**: Search engines follow links to browse the 60 trillion (and growing) pages that make up the web.
2.  **Sorting**: Results are sorted by a variety of factors (more on this in a second!).
3.  **Indexing**: Sorted pages are stored in an index so search engines can match queries with results faster.
