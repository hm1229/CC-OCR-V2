Actually, **motorcycle accidents do not cause suicides** — they are a separate category of death.

Looking at the infographic:

- On the left side, under **“Leading Causes of Death for 15–24 Year Olds Each Year”**, it lists:
  - **Suicide: 23.3%**
  - Car Accidents: 14%
  - Drugs and Poisoning: 4.2%
  - **Motorcycle Accidents: 4.2%**
  - Pedestrian Accidents: 2.9%
  - Cancer: 2.4%

These are **separate causes of death**, not subcategories of suicide.

The central message — “**Suicide accounts for one in four deaths among young people**” — refers to the fact that **23.3% is roughly 1 in 4** (since 1/4 = 25%).

So, to answer your question directly:

> **0%** of suicides are caused by motorcycle accidents — because motorcycle accidents are an *alternative* cause of death, not a cause *of* suicide.

They are listed alongside suicide as competing leading causes of death in this age group.
