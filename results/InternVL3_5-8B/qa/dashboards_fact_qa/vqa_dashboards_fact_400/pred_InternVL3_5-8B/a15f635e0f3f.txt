In 2003, 57.2% of academic leaders believed that online education was the same or even superior to face-to-face learning. By 2005, that number increased to 77%.

The increase is:

77% - 57.2% = 19.8%

So, 19.8% more academic leaders found online education to be the same or even superior to face-to-face learning in 2005 compared to 2003.
