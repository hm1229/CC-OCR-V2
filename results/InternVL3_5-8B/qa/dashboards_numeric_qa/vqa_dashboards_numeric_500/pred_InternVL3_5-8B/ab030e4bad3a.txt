Abraham Lincoln became the president of the United States in 1861, following the election of 1860. His election was a significant factor in the lead-up to the Civil War, as it prompted Southern states to fear that their way of life, particularly the institution of slavery, would be threatened. Lincoln's presidency was marked by his efforts to preserve the Union and his issuance of the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, which declared that all slaves in Confederate-held territory were to be set free.
