Based on the infographic “**Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee: 60 Years of Population Changes in Australia**” by McCrindle Research, here’s what it says about the **85+ ageing population**:

---

### 📊 **The Ageing Population (85+)**

- **1952**:  
  → **30,506** people aged 85 and over  
  *(Visually represented as ~30,000 with a single icon of an elderly person with a cane)*

- **2012**:  
  → **415,331** people aged 85 and over  
  *(Visually represented by 14 icons of elderly people with canes — symbolizing “nearly 14x as many”)*

---

### 🔢 Key Statistic:
> **“That’s nearly 14x as many!”**

This means the number of Australians aged 85+ increased by approximately **13.6 times** between 1952 and 2012.

---

### 💡 Context:
This dramatic growth reflects broader demographic trends:
- Increased life expectancy
- Aging of the post-WWII baby boomers
- Improved healthcare and living conditions

The infographic uses this data to highlight how Australia’s population has not only grown overall but also significantly aged over the 60-year period coinciding with Queen Elizabeth II’s reign.
