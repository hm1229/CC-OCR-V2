Based on the infographic provided, the "Education First" initiative, launched by former UN Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon, is built upon three primary pillars:

**1. Put every child in school**
*   **The Challenge:** 61 million primary-aged children are out of school, with 32 million being girls and over 28 million living in conflict-affected countries.
*   **The Goal:** Eliminate cost barriers, provide nutritional and health support, close the gender gap, build 4 million more classrooms, and double the percentage of humanitarian aid dedicated to education.

**2. Improve the quality of learning**
*   **The Challenge:** 250 million children cannot read, write, or count well, even after four years of schooling.
*   **The Goal:** Recruit 2 million more well-trained teachers, improve traditional and digital learning materials in students' native languages, increase early-childhood education programs by 45%, and better align schooling with workforce skills.

**3. Foster global citizenship**
*   **The Challenge:** The world faces global challenges that require global solutions, necessitating an education system that cultivates a sense of shared responsibility.
*   **The Goal:** Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity; cultivate a sense of community, active participation, and environmental stewardship; and train teachers to use innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

**Additionally, the initiative highlights the need to "Close the Financial Gap":**
*   **The Goal:** Generate additional funding through advocacy, increase global investment in poor countries' school systems by $24 billion per year, and encourage countries to invest at least 5–6% of their national GDP in education.
