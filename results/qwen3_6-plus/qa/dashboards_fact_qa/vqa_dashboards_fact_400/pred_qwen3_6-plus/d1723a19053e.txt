The original name of **Sister Nivedita** was **Margaret Elizabeth Noble**.

She was an Irish teacher, author, and social activist who became a disciple of Swami Vivekananda. Born in Ireland in 1867, she moved to India in 1898 and took the name “Nivedita” (meaning “dedicated to God”) upon her initiation into the Ramakrishna Order. She played a significant role in education, women’s rights, and the Indian independence movement — as noted in the infographic, which mentions her prominence alongside figures like Margaret Cousins and Annie Besant.

So, to directly answer your question:

> **Margaret Elizabeth Noble**

*(Sometimes referred to simply as Margaret Noble before her spiritual renaming.)*
