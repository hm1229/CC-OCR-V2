Based on the information at the bottom of the infographic, the fatality rate is calculated as follows:

**The formula is: the number of fatal injuries divided by number of hours worked by all employees during the calendar year, then multiplied by 200,000,000.**

This is based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics formula, and the rate is expressed per 100,000 full-time workers or equivalent. The 200,000,000 figure represents the equivalent of 100,000 workers working 2,000 hours per year (100,000 × 2,000 = 200,000,000), which standardizes the rate to a full-time worker basis for comparison purposes.
