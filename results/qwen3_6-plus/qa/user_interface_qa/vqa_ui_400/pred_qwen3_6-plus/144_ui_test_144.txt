In the **Decentralised** section of the infographic (left side), there are **4 numbered steps** shown:

1. The app broadcasts a random ID and collects IDs from other apps it interacts with.
2. If a user reports they are unwell, they can choose to upload their ID to a central database.
3. Users’ phones regularly download the most up-to-date version of this database and look for IDs they have interacted with.
4. If they see any ID(s) they have interacted with, their app calculates whether or not to inform them that they have been in contact with an infected person.

✅ So, the answer is: **4 steps**

*(Note: Although step 2 mentions uploading to a “central database,” this is still part of the decentralised model — the key difference is that risk calculation happens on-device in step 4, not centrally.)*
