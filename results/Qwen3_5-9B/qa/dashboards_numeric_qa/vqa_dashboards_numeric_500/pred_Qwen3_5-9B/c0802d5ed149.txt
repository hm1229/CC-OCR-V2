Looking at the map titled **“The United States of Turkey”** from Cabela’s State of the Union 2015:

- The **blue** color (representing **Eastern Wild Turkey**) covers a **major contiguous area** across the eastern half of the U.S., including states like Missouri, Alabama, and much of the Northeast and Southeast.
- The **red/orange** colors (Rio Grande Wild Turkey) are concentrated in the Southwest and parts of Texas — significant but not as widespread as blue.
- The **green** (Hybrid Turkey) appears in scattered patches, mostly in the central U.S.
- The **purple** (Merriam’s Wild Turkey) is in the West, but again, not as dominant as blue.

✅ So, among the options given — **red, orange, blue, or green** — the color that covers the **major area** of the map is:

> **Blue**

*(Note: While red and orange are grouped together in your question, they represent different subspecies and cover less total area than blue.)*
