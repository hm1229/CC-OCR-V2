## Internet Access Gap in Education - Key Findings

Based on the infographic, here are the detailed answers:

### What Students Do Online (Top 5 activities):
1. **95%** - Research or search for information online
2. **79%** - Access or download assignments from an online site
3. **76%** - Submit assignments online
4. **40%** - Develop, share, or post their work on a website, wiki, or blog
5. **39%** - Participate in online discussions

### Access to Internet/Digital Technologies at Home:

Looking at the **"Access to Internet Digital Technologies"** section under **"At Home"**:

- **3%** - All or almost all
- **54%** - Most
- **28%** - Only some
- **2%** - Hardly any

So **3%** of students have access to all (or almost all) kinds of digital technologies at their home.

### Highlights:
- **Four-Fifths (80%)** of K-12 schools have insufficient Internet for their current needs
- **35%** of households earning less than $25,000 have broadband access to the Internet
- **94%** of households with income in excess of $100,000 have broadband access
- **15%** of teachers of upper income students say their school is "behind the curve"
- **39%** of teachers of low-income students say their school is "behind the curve"
