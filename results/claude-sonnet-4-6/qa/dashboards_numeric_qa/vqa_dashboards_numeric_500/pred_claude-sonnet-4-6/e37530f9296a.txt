## Fatality Rate Calculation

According to the footnote mentioned in the infographic, the fatality rate is calculated as:

> **Per 100,000 full-time workers in an occupation**, based on the **Bureau of Labor Statistics' formula**.

This is a standard occupational fatality rate metric, meaning it represents the number of **fatal work injuries for every 100,000 full-time equivalent workers** in a given occupation during a specific period.

This standardized rate allows for **fair comparisons** between occupations of vastly different sizes — for example, comparing a small occupation like logging workers to a much larger one like construction laborers.
