The **Laboratory test** is the antibody test that involves blood testing by a medical professional.

As shown in the infographic:

- Under the **Antibody test** section (green header: “Have I had it?”), there are two subtypes:
  - **Laboratory test**: Described as “Blood test taken by a medical professional” — illustrated with a healthcare worker drawing blood from a patient’s arm.
  - **Home test**: Described as “‘Finger-prick’ test taken at home” — illustrated with a person pricking their own finger.

So, the correct answer is:  
> **Laboratory test**

This test is currently being offered to NHS and social care staff, hospital inpatients, and care home residents based on clinician advice — but not yet available to the general public due to accuracy concerns.
