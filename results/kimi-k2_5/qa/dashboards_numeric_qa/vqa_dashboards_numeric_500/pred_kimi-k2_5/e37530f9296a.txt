Based on the information at the bottom of the infographic, the fatality rate is calculated using the Bureau of Labor Statistics formula:

**The number of fatal injuries divided by the number of hours worked by all employees during the calendar year, then multiplied by 200,000,000.**

This produces a rate per 100,000 full-time workers or equivalent. The 200,000,000 figure represents the standard base of 100,000 workers working 2,000 hours per year (100,000 × 2,000 = 200,000,000), which allows for comparison across different occupations and industries.
