The candidate for president is chosen at the National Party Conventions. Here's a detailed breakdown of the process:

1. **Election Process**:
   - The election process involves the Electoral College, where each state has a certain number of electors based on its representation in Congress (House of Representatives and Senate).

2. **Electoral College**:
   - The number of electors each state has depends on its population. Larger states have more electors.
   - To win the presidency, a candidate must secure at least 270 electoral votes out of the total 538.

3. **National Party Conventions**:
   - The National Party Conventions are where the party officially nominates its candidate for president.
   - Delegates from each state gather to cast their votes for the party's nominee.

4. **Swing States**:
   - The infographic highlights the importance of "swing states" or battleground states where the outcome is uncertain, and thus, candidates focus their campaigns here.

5. **Presidential Terms**:
   - There have been 44 U.S. Presidents, with 20 having served more than one term.
   - Franklin D. Roosevelt is the only president to have served four terms.
   - Stephen Grover Cleveland is the only U.S. president to have served non-consecutive terms.

6. **Voter Turnout**:
   - The infographic also shows voter turnout percentages in presidential elections from 1964 to 2008, indicating fluctuations over the years.

7. **Demographics**:
   - It provides data on the age, sex, and ethnic group demographics of voters in the 2008 presidential election.

This structured approach ensures that the candidate is chosen through a combination of state-level support and national party conventions, reflecting both popular and electoral votes.
