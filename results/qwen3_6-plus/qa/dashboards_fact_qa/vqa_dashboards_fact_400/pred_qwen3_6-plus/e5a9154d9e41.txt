Based on the infographic provided, **infrared touchscreens are more durable than resistive touchscreens**.

Here’s the breakdown from the “DURABILITY” row:

- **Resistive**:  
  > *Fair Durability. Screens are vulnerable to scratches and other minor damage. The screen will wear over time and need replacing.*

- **Infrared (both heat-sensitive and optical)**:  
  > *Good Durability. Glass shatters upon major impact only.*

### Key Takeaway:
While both infrared types use glass (which can shatter under major impact), they are not prone to surface wear or scratching like resistive screens. Resistive screens rely on flexible layers that degrade with repeated pressure and are easily scratched — making them less durable overall.

✅ So, **infrared > resistive** in terms of durability according to this chart.

*(Note: Capacitive is also rated “Good Durability,” same as infrared — but your question specifically compares resistive vs. infrared.)*
