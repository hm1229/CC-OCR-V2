\section*{A proverbial Phrase.}

\begin{quote}
Dea dombvída vöga dsa vidla vöga pësa (or passion) vöga. Given with following explanation: Easter-Sunday weather will last all the week. More correct probably: the weather of Passion-week will be the weather of Easter-week.
\end{quote}

The Coningsburgh phrase (acc. to Low): “Myrk in Liora, Luce in Liunga, Tim in e Guest in e geungna” (It’s dark in the Chimney, but it’s light thro’ the Heath, it’s still time for the stranger to be gone).

In Yell (in “de Herra”) this phrase is still preserved in the following form:

\begin{quote}
Mörka lóra, lestra hnga, tåmra grætra gongara [myrkt er i ljóra, ljóst er i lyngi, timi er, at gest(rinn) gengr]. With regard to the form gongara, cf. Fær. gongur.
\end{quote}

Our Lord’s Prayer\footnote{A man still living in Unst was formerly able, according to his own and other’s statements, to recite Our Lord’s Prayer in Norn, or at any rate, the greater part of it. Some time ago, however, he had an illness which gradually enfeebled his memory, and when I looked him up, he could only remember the following corrupted fragments: — \textit{halagot war’ti name} (hallowed be Thy name) — see himblins (as in Heaven).} from Foula (reported by Low in his “Tour thro’ Orkney and Shetland”, reprinted by Barry and in “Annaler for nordisk oldkyndighed”, 1860):

\begin{quote}
Fy vor o er i Chimeri. Halaght vara nam det. La Konungdum din cumma. La vill din vera gærdh i vridlin sinda eri Chimeri. Gav vs dagh u dagloght brau. Forgive sindor wasa (wara?) sin vi for-giva gem ao sinda galmst was. Lia was eke o vera tempa, bet de-livra was fro adlu idlu, fro do i ir Konungdum, u puri, u glori. Amen.
\end{quote}

\section*{An Incantation.}

This is found in the tale: “Da Tief i’ de Neean” (Shetland Times 1879).

The tale itself, as well as the language used in it, doubtless originates from Al. (Wests).

The formula, which is mainly in Scoto-English, has Norn words sprinkled throughout, some of which show old grammatical endings, that have lost their significance. It runs as follows:

\begin{quote}
Da stuhl es scarp an fien, \\
Da sacheles emer a snean, \\
Da vird es sicer en pura, \\
A glimer i’ mirk-as-dim hura, \\
La stuhl an vird ay gyrdra \\
An prof er an skyia, an svirda.
\end{quote}
