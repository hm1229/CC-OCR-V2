The bread cooked over a damped fire’s ashes is **Damper**.

As described in the infographic:

> **Damping**  
> A fire is damped to allow the bread to be cooked in the fire’s ashes – hence the name.

Damper is a traditional Australian soda bread, originally prepared by swagmen, drovers, and stockmen as a travelling food. Its name comes directly from this cooking method — “damping” the fire to create hot ashes for baking.

So, the answer is: **Damper**.
