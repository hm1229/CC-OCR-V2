Based on the infographic, **infrared** touch screens are more durable than resistive ones.

Here’s why, according to the “DURABILITY” row:

- **Resistive**:  
  > “Fair Durability. Screens are vulnerable to scratches and other minor damage. The screen will wear over time and need replacing.”

- **Infrared**:  
  > “Good Durability. Glass shatters upon major impact only.”

This means:
- Resistive screens can be damaged by everyday wear (scratches, pressure, etc.) and may degrade over time.
- Infrared screens use glass that only breaks under significant impact — implying better resistance to daily handling and wear.

✅ So, **infrared is more durable** than resistive.

*(Note: Capacitive is also rated “Good Durability” with similar glass behavior, but since your question was between resistive and infrared, infrared wins.)*
