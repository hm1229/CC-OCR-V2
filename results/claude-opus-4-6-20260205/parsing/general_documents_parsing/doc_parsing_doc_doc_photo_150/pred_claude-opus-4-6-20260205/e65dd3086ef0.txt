The standard form of a linear line is $Y = mx + b$ where $m$ is the slope and $b$ is the \textbf{y-intercept}.

The questions for this week is, convert the following linear equation into the standard form, and find the \textbf{y-intercept}.

$$3x + 5 = 7y$$

\bigskip

Answer the following:

1. Suppose you are given two points on the plane, explain briefly how to find the equation of the straight link passing through the two points.

2. Suppose two straight lines ($Y_1 = m_1 x + b_1$; $Y_2 = m_2 x + b_2$) are perpendicular to each other, what can you say about the slopes?

\bigskip

Look at an example of a polynomial $7x^3 - 2x^2 + x - 5$. We say that the degree of this polynomial is 3, which is the highest power.

For this week's discussion, briefly explain how the degree of a divider is compared to the degree of the remainder. Divide the following expression, and compare the degree of the divider: $x + 3$ and the degree of the remainder:

$$\frac{(7x^3 - 2x^2 + x - 5)}{x + 3}$$

\bigskip

Does a system of linear equations always have a solution? What about the following system?

$$x + 3y = 1, \quad 2x + 6y = 3$$

If we think of the two equations in the above system as two ``straight lines'' in the plane, can you explain why the system doesn't have a solution?

\bigskip

Explain how to graph an inequality on the plane? Use the following example: $9x - 5y \geq 15$

What steps are needed to graph it? How would you go about showing someone else how to do it?
