Crack Shot, brother of Kgosidintsi,
he and Paul shot at each other all day;
at midday Paul became tired,
Crack Shot alone remained shooting.²
¹ < Afr. klip, rock, stone.
² Kgosidintsi was Sechele's half-brother and leading adviser; 'Paul' was Paul
Kruger, who took part in the attack on Dimawe. 'Marksman' would be a more
appropriate rendering for Rramokonopi than 'Crack Shot', but the latter brings out
the word play in lines 2 and 4 (Rramokonopi and -konopana, line 2, are both deriva-
tives of -konopa, line 4).
