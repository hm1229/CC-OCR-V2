STAIR An Béal Bocht 109

Nuair a tháining sé amach i ndéiradh na dálta tóghadh ceann de i gcéin is i gcóngar. Scriobhadh léirmheasanna air go foreleathan,10 iad uile nach mó r moltach. Ba mó ná sásta a bhí an t-údar ar ndóigh:

I am rather pleased at the reception given to my book, 'An Béal Bocht'. It is gratifying to know that an important work of literature receives in this country the recognition that is its due. Scholars, students, men-about-town, clerics, T.D.s, ladies of fashion, and even the better-class corner-boys have vied with one another in grabbing the copies as they pour from the giant presses. How long will the strictly limited edition of 50,000 copies last? A week? A month? Who can tell? Suffice it to say that you cannot order your copy too soon. Paper difficulties make it doubtful whether another edition of 50,000 will be possible, in our own generation at any rate . . . 

About the book itself, it is indeed, fine stuff. It is the first thing of the kind ever published.

You can look out any day now for a deluge of imitations. Refuse all substitutes. Every genuine copy bears the name, 'Myles na gCopaleen'. (CLn 12/12/1941)

Ag Féachtint Cluathnach

'An Béal Bocht. Ní amhras na gur bhast ughdar an leabhar seo thas a leabhar féin maith go leor: is fearta dún gan tabhairt fá n-a bhréagnughadh, acht d'fhág sé an nídh amháin gan luadh - do b' é ba chór do a ráth 'droch-seagal ar an droch-shaoighal, brecáite i ndroch-Ghaedhil!' 

'Ná measadh éinne nár léighéas-sa An Béal Bocht, mar do léighéas, agus san le mó r-churam, gach aon fhoical de - mar phionós! Do chaithesas im óige roinn bhliadhanta thar leár, agus tharla dhám, tráth, i gcursai an tsaoighal, siubhal trasna tanuís (sic) (monarcha sudairéacht) agus lá eile trasna monarchan ceimice - do bhí bóthar príobháideach i ngach ait aca: fágaim le h-uadhacht go raibh orm rith trasna ar mo dhicheall (sic) agus greim daingean agam ar pholláiríb mo choinnicin,* sara dtuitinn leis an mbalaithe. Ní mó r ná gur bh' é an dálta ceadna agam é agus mé ag léighéamh i dtaoibh na muice bréine sin insan Bhéal Bocht!
