This document is a **Universal Credit Card Charge Form** issued by **Eastern Air Lines**.

In the context of the airline industry, this is specifically an **Air Travel Card** charge form. Before the widespread use of general-purpose credit cards (like Visa or Mastercard) for travel, airlines used a proprietary system called the "Air Travel Card." This allowed businesses to issue cards to employees for travel expenses, which were then billed directly to the company.

*   **The Issuer:** Eastern Air Lines (indicated by the logo and name at the top).
*   **The Purpose:** It served as the official record of a ticket purchase, acting as both a receipt and a billing authorization for the credit card company or the corporate account.

These forms were typically printed on multi-part carbon paper so that the airline, the credit card clearinghouse, and the passenger each received a copy of the transaction.
