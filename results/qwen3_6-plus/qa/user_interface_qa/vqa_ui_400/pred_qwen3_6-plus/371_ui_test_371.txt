Actually, **zero** appliances suck up electricity when *not plugged in*.

The infographic refers to **“Vampire Appliances”** — which are devices that consume electricity **while plugged in but switched off or in standby mode**. The key phrase is:  
> “Appliances and gadgets that suck up electricity while plugged in even though switched off.”

So, if an appliance is **unplugged**, it draws **no power** — vampire draw only happens when the device is still connected to the outlet, even if turned “off.”

---

### List of Vampire Appliances shown in the infographic:
1. Flat Screen TVs  
2. Gaming Console  
3. Computers  
4. DVRs / Cable Boxes  
5. DVD / VCR  
6. Space Heater *(note: some space heaters may have digital displays or timers that draw standby power)*  
7. Phones *(chargers left plugged in without phones can still draw small amounts)*  
8. Printer  
9. Router / Modem  
10. Kitchen Appliances *(e.g., microwaves with clocks, coffee makers with displays)*

That’s **10 categories** of vampire appliances listed.

---

### Important Clarification:
- **“Not plugged in” = 0 energy use**
- **“Plugged in but off/standby” = vampire energy use**

So, to answer your question literally:  
> **How many appliances suck up electricity even when not plugged in?**  
✅ **Answer: 0**

But if you meant:  
> **How many types of appliances are listed as “vampire appliances” (i.e., draw power when plugged in but off)?**  
✅ **Answer: 10**
