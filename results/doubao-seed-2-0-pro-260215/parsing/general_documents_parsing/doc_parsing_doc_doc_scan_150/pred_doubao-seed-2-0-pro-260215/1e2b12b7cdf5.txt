\section*{5 \quad PRESENT PERFECT}
\noindent
\begin{tabular}{|>{\bfseries}l|p{9cm}|}
\hline
Form & have or has + Past Participle \\
\hline
Use & For actions which started in the past and continued up to the present. \\
\hline
Signal words & just, already, yet, for, since, so far, recently, ever... \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\vspace{10pt}
\noindent NB. We use Present Perfect Continuous (have/has+ been + verb +ing) to give more emphasis on the duration of the action. However, some verbs can't be used in the continuous form such as own, smell, need, belong, cost, agree, contain..., etc.

\vspace{10pt}
\noindent\fbox{\textbf{EXERCISE 1}} \quad \textbf{Find the signal words for present perfect.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Which is a signal word for present perfect?\\
    still – usually – since
    \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present perfect?\\
    since – before – for
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{10pt}
