The area between the wickets on a cricket pitch is called the "pitch" or "strip." It is the area where the batsmen play the ball and where the bowler delivers the ball. The pitch is typically a rectangular area of grass, and its dimensions can vary slightly depending on the specific ground and the type of match being played. The pitch is usually marked with lines to indicate the boundaries and the crease, which are important for determining whether the batsmen are out or not. The pitch is also where the stumps and bails are placed, and it is the central focus of the game.
