The long-term benefits of early childhood education fall into two broad categories:
---
### 1. Student success
- Recipients are 29% more likely to graduate from high school
- They are 44% less likely to need to repeat a grade
- They pass standardized tests more frequently
---
### 2. Adult responsibility / positive adult outcomes
- By age 40, people who received early childhood education are more likely to be employed, and have a 33% higher average income than peers who did not receive this education
- *Not* receiving early childhood education makes a person 70% more likely to be arrested for a violent crime by age 18, so early childhood education drastically reduces this risk.
