To find the percentage of free apps in the **iPhone** and **Android** app stores combined, we need to look at the "Heavy Hitters" section of the infographic, which shows the breakdown of paid vs. free apps for each platform.

- **Apple App Store (iPhone):**

  - Free apps: **41%**

We are asked to find the **combined percentage of free apps** in both stores. However, since these percentages are from different stores with potentially different total numbers of apps, we cannot simply average them unless we assume equal weight. But the question seems to be asking for a combined proportion based on the data provided.

Let’s assume we are combining the **free app percentages** as a weighted average based on the number of apps in each store.

From earlier in the infographic:
- **Apple App Store:** 375,566 apps
- **Android Market:** 224,808 apps

Total apps = 375,566 + 224,808 = **600,374**

Now calculate the **total number of free apps**:
- Free apps on iPhone: 29% of 375,566 =  
  $ 0.29 \times 375,566 = 108,914.14 $
- Free apps on Android: 41% of 224,808 =  
  $ 0.41 \times 224,808 = 92,171.28 $

Total free apps = 108,914.14 + 92,171.28 = **201,085.42**

Now, the **percentage of free apps** across both stores:
$$
\frac{201,085.42}{600,374} \times 100 \approx 33.5\%
$$
