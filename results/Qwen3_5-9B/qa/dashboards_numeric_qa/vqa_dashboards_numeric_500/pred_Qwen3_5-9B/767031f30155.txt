Based on the infographic titled **“COVID-19: Canada’s Economic Response Plan”**, here are the emergency benefits provided to working Canadians:

---

### 1. **Emergency Care Benefit**
- **Purpose**: For those who must stay home due to COVID-19 and don’t have paid sick leave.
- **Eligibility**: May include individuals who:
  - Can’t access Employment Insurance (EI)
  - Are sick
  - Are taking care of a sick child or someone who is sick
- **Amount**: Up to **$900 bi-weekly**
- **Duration**: Up to **15 weeks**

---

### 2. **Emergency Support Benefit**
- **Purpose**: To support workers who are not eligible for EI and are facing unemployment or reduced hours.
- **Amount**: Up to **$5 billion** in total support (note: this is the *total funding allocated*, not per person)

---

### Additional Support for Working Canadians:
- **$27 billion** in direct support for Canadian workers and businesses
- **$55 billion** in support for business liquidity through tax deferrals

---

These measures were part of Canada’s broader economic response to mitigate the impact of the pandemic on individuals and businesses.

> 💡 *Note: The “$5 billion” under Emergency Support Benefit refers to the total program funding, not an individual payment amount.*
