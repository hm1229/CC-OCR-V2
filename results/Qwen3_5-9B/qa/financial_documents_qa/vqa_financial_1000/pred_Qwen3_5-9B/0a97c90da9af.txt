Yugoslavia pardons **Lazlo Toth**, a resident of Thornton, Colorado.

He was arrested in Yugoslavia in August 1975 for “industrial espionage” after allegedly taking photographs of a beet-sugar plant in Vrbas, a suburb of Belgrade. He was sentenced to seven years in prison after a closed-door trial.

On Thursday (the article is dated Friday, July 16, 1976), it was announced that Toth had been pardoned by the Yugoslav collective presidency — a nine-member ruling body that includes President Josip Broz Tito. The pardon was confirmed by the U.S. Embassy in Belgrade.

Toth, 46 at the time, is a naturalized American citizen and former employee of Great Western Sugar Co. in Loveland, Colorado. His wife, Zora, expressed relief and happiness at the news, though she noted they still needed confirmation of his release date before he could return to the United States.

The pardon appears to be part of President Tito’s annual tradition of granting executive clemency to a few prisoners on his birthday.
