For each of the following formulae, choose the sentence that provides the best English translation of
the formula according to the following symbolization key:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|l|}
\hline
$B$ & Bob is gathering eggs. \\
\hline
$C$ & Bob has fed the chickens. \\
\hline
$D$ & Bob has fed the ducks. \\
\hline
$F$ & Bob is chasing the fox away. \\
\hline
$P$ & The ducks are playing in the pond. \\
\hline
$Q$ & The ducks are quacking loudly. \\
\hline
$S$ & The chickens are playing in the pond. \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsubsection*{Question 10}
\noindent Formula: \((F \rightarrow (Q \& \neg S))\)
\hfill \begin{minipage}{3cm}\raggedright Select one answer.\\2 points\end{minipage}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[A.] $\bigcirc$ If the ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond, then Bob is chasing the fox away.
    \item[B.] $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond unless Bob is chasing the fox away.
    \item[C.] $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond if Bob is chasing the fox away.
    \item[D.] $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and the chickens aren't playing in the pond only if Bob is chasing the fox away.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Question 11}
\noindent Formula: \(((P \& Q) \& S)\)
\hfill \begin{minipage}{3cm}\raggedright Select one answer.\\2 points\end{minipage}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[A.] $\bigcirc$ The ducks are quacking loudly and playing in the pond, and the chickens are also playing in the pond.
\end{enumerate}
