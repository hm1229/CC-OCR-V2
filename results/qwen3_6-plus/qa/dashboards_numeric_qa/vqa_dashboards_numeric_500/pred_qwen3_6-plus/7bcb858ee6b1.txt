According to the **Barbados Ministry of Health and Wellness COVID-19 Situation Report** (Friday, June 05, 2020), the recommended time to properly sanitize your hands is:

> **20 seconds**

This is shown in the infographic under the “Wash your hands” section — specifically in the step that says:

> **“Rub the backs of your fingers on opposing palms with fingers interlocked”**  
> and also in the final step:  
> **“Use the towel to turn off the faucet”** — both accompanied by a **“20s”** icon.

Additionally, for alcohol-based hand sanitizer (when soap and water aren’t available), the report advises:

> *“Even if your hands are not visibly dirty, frequently clean them using a 60% or higher alcohol-based sanitizer gel.”*

While it doesn’t specify exact timing for sanitizer use, public health guidelines (including WHO and CDC) generally recommend rubbing sanitizer over all surfaces of the hands until they are dry — which typically takes about **20 seconds** as well.

✅ **Final Answer: 20 seconds**
