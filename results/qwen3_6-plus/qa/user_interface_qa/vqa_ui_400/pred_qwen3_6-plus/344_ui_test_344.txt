During the **Battle of Zama (204 B.C.)**, **Carthage** had more infantry than Rome.

According to the infographic:

- **Romans**: 34,000 infantry  
- **Carthage**: 50,000 infantry

✅ So, **Carthage** had **16,000 more infantry** than Rome at the start of the battle.

---

### Additional Context from the Infographic:
Although Carthage had superior numbers in infantry (and also fielded 80 war elephants), the Romans under Scipio Africanus used clever tactics — such as opening channels in their lines to let the elephants pass through harmlessly — and ultimately won the battle thanks to superior cavalry maneuvering (especially the Numidian cavalry) and disciplined legionary formations.

The final outcome was a decisive Roman victory, ending the Second Punic War.
