Based on the infographic “The Benefits of Early Childhood Education,” the **long-term benefits** are divided into two main categories:

---

### 1. **Student Success**
- Students with early childhood education are **29% more likely to graduate from high school**.
- They are **44% less likely to repeat a grade**.
- They **pass standardized tests more frequently**.

---

### 2. **Adult Responsibility**
- Adults age 40 who had early childhood education are **more likely to be employed**.
- These same adults have a **33% higher average income**.
- 18-year-olds **without** early childhood education are **70% more likely to be arrested for a violent crime** (implying that those *with* early education are significantly less likely to be involved in violent crime).

---

✅ In summary, the long-term benefits include:
- Higher educational attainment and academic performance
- Greater employment rates and income in adulthood
- Reduced likelihood of criminal behavior

These outcomes highlight how early childhood education lays a foundation for lifelong success and social responsibility.

*(Source: Campbell Christian Schools, citing data from Early Childhood Alliance, KUEducation, and HighScope)*
