SECHELE

nku gadike dimmuthwa dilepedi.
Hela, mothakane ofetang,
ontse omomomela-momomela,
ore, Kgomedinaké, omntlogeléle,
oyé gumba meuba,
nku gadike dimmuthwa dilepedi.
Setshéle, naya mpya dikata,
edikaté pele eretlogéle.
A golo kwagéno kgosi gadiyó,
yare Moditsho thlaka dibina agotshware?
Kana Setshéle obokilwé adiré eng?
Ntswa elegóne okabó abokwa,
motshitsi a bómma leborara;
maloba ja Dimawé ontshitshute,
ontshitshitse fa Senamako léng;
banna bamose wa bóré wa bó omatha.
Dikokwé kesenka Rramatlhabuswane,
kare, gagana lamaabane lekáe,
lérelebóneyeng reé ja mopudu,
a fa jaána garekalefeté?
Tshéshébé tsanoka ya Pólo,
tsanoka ya gá Rramaphathi,
tsatlóga tsáéma dipaphó,
tsáéma tsaphaka masélo,
tsaba tsáamusa ditsétalétse.
Kékonopétse tshiló bodibéng,
mokelepéng' ya Mmaségaísó,
kaba kataya motshwaíse kaífisa,
kataya katebáteba kamóleló kasesikalaba.
Réboka Thibadi vénó,
Rradísébé wa Matómélse le Kágakge.

Crack Shot, brother of Kgosi dintsí,
he and Paul shot at each other all day;
at midday Paul became tired,
Crack Shot alone remained shooting.¹

¹ < Afr. klíp, rock, stone.
² Kgosi dintsí was Sechele's half-brother and leading adviser; 'Paul' was Paul Kruger, who took part in the attack on Dimawé. 'Marksman' would be a more appropriate rendering for Rramokonopi than 'Crack Shot', but the latter brings out the word play in lines 2 and 4 (Rramokonopi and -konopana, line 2, are both derivatives of -konopa, line 4).
