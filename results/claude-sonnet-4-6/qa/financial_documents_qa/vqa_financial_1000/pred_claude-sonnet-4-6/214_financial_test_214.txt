## Table 1: Class Performance Recorded After Taking the Course on Food and Nutrition

The table presents **class performance data** from **1975 and 1976 classes**, measuring the **percentage of students agreeing with course teachings** across different categories of questions:

| Category | 1975 Class | 1976 Class |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| 17 questions relating to established economic or scientific facts | 88% | 86% |
| 17 questions relating to confirmed scientific findings but still rejected by consumer activists | 82% | 78% |
| 16 questions relating to issues pitting professional scientists' opinions against consumer activists | 83% | 80% |
| **Entire test (50 questions)** | **84%** | **81%** |

### Key Observations:
- The **1975 class** consistently showed **higher agreement** with course teachings than the 1976 class across all categories.
- The highest agreement was for **established economic or scientific facts** (88% in 1975, 86% in 1976).
- The lowest agreement was for questions about **confirmed scientific findings rejected by consumer activists** (82% in 1975, 78% in 1976).
