Based on the infographic, Ban Ki-moon's initiatives as United Nations Secretary-General are the **"Education First"** initiative, which has three main priorities:

## The Three Initiatives of Education First:

1. **Put every child in school**
   - Focus on getting the 61 million primary-aged children who are out of school into education
   - Eliminate cost barriers, close gender gaps, and build more classrooms

2. **Improve the quality of learning**
   - Address the 250 million children who cannot read, write or count well despite attending school
   - Train more teachers, improve learning materials, and increase early-childhood education

3. **Foster global citizenship**
   - Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity
   - Cultivate community participation and environmental stewardship
   - Train teachers in innovative, participatory teaching methods

## Additional Priority:

4. **Close the financial gap**
   - Generate sufficient funding for education
   - Increase global investment by $24 billion per year in poor countries' school systems
   - Invest at least 5-6% of countries' national GDP in education

These initiatives aim to reduce poverty and hunger, end wasted potential, and build stronger, more prosperous, healthy, and equitable societies through education.
