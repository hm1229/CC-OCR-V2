Based on the infographic for the **Battle of Blore Heath (1459)**, the **Lancastrians** had significantly more strength in terms of troop numbers.

Here’s the breakdown from the image:

- **Lancastrians**:  
  - Commanders: Baron Audley / Baron Dudley  
  - Men: **8,000 – 14,000**

- **Yorkists**:  
  - Commander: Earl of Salisbury  
  - Men: **3,000 – 5,000**

Even at their lowest estimate (8,000), the Lancastrians outnumbered the Yorkists (max 5,000) by a ratio of roughly **1.6 to 1**. At their highest estimates (14,000 vs. 3,000), the advantage was over **4 to 1**.

---

### Important Note:
Despite being heavily outnumbered, the **Yorkists won the battle** through superior tactics — specifically, feigning defeat to lure the Lancastrian cavalry into a charge, then slaughtering them from an uphill position. The infographic notes that 500 Lancastrians even deserted during the battle.

So while the **Lancastrians had more strength (numbers)**, the **Yorkists achieved victory through strategy and terrain**.

✅ **Answer: The Lancastrians had more strength (troop numbers) during the Battle of Blore Heath.**
