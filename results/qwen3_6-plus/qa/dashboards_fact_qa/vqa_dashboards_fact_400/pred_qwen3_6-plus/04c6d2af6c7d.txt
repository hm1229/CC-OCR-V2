According to the infographic, at the **1909 Seattle World’s Fair**, people queued for hours to see premature babies displayed in newly-invented incubators — and they **paid 25 cents** for the privilege.

> “People queued for hours to see the tiny babies and paid 25 cents for the privilege.”

So, the fee was **25 cents**.

*(Note: This practice, while shocking by today’s standards, was actually a real historical phenomenon — incubator exhibits were popular attractions at fairs and amusement parks in the early 20th century, often run by doctors like Dr. Martin Couney, who used them to fund neonatal care.)*
