Based on the provided infographic titled **“Education First”**, Ban Ki-moon’s initiative is a global campaign to prioritize education as a fundamental human right and a key driver of development. The core pillars of his **“Education First”** initiative are:

---

### 🎯 **Three Main Goals:**

1. **Put every child in school**  
   - Address the 61 million primary-aged children out of school (32 million girls, 28 million in conflict-affected poor countries).  
   - Eliminate cost barriers, close gender gaps, build classrooms in rural/conflict areas, and provide nutritional/health support.

2. **Improve the quality of learning**  
   - 250 million children cannot read, write, or count well — even after four years of school.  
   - Train 2 million more teachers, improve learning materials, increase early-childhood education programs, and align schooling with workforce needs.

3. **Foster global citizenship**  
   - Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity.  
   - Cultivate community, active participation, and environmental stewardship.  
   - Train teachers in innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

---

### 💰 **Financial Commitment:**

- **Close the financial gap**:  
  - Invest at least **5–6% of national GDP** in education (currently inadequate in many countries).  
  - Increase global investment in poor countries’ school systems by **$24 billion per year**.  
  - Ensure effective and accountable use of resources aligned with national education plans.

---

### 🌍 **Vision & Impact:**

> *“When we put Education First, we can reduce poverty and hunger, end wasted potential—and look forward to stronger and better societies for all.”*  
> — Ban Ki-moon

> *“Education lifts people out of poverty. If all students in low-income countries acquire basic reading skills, 171 million people could be lifted out of poverty, equivalent to a 12 percent cut in world poverty.”*  
> — Ban Ki-moon

---

### ✅ **Key Actions Required (“WE MUST” / “WE NEED”):**

- Eliminate cost barriers to schooling.
- Close the gender gap.
- Build 4 million more classrooms.
- Double humanitarian aid’s percentage of education funding.
- Improve teacher training and learning materials.
- Foster peace, tolerance, and global citizenship through education.

---

This initiative is supported by the United Nations Secretary-General’s Education First Initiative, UNESCO, and UNICEF, aiming to ensure that every child has access to quality education — not just enrollment, but meaningful learning and empowerment.

📌 **Learn more**: [globaleducationfirst.org](http://globaleducationfirst.org)

--- 

In summary, Ban Ki-moon’s “Education First” initiative is a comprehensive, multi-faceted global effort to ensure **access, quality, and equity** in education worldwide — recognizing it as essential for poverty reduction, peace, and sustainable development.
