Sodium helps regulate water in the human body by **controlling the transfer of water and other nutrients across cell membranes**.

This is a key part of maintaining fluid balance — sodium acts as an osmotic regulator, meaning it draws water with it. When sodium levels are high in a particular area (like outside a cell), water moves toward that area to balance the concentration. This mechanism helps:

- Maintain proper hydration inside and outside cells
- Regulate blood volume and pressure (since blood is mostly water)
- Support nerve impulse transmission and muscle function (which depend on precise fluid and electrolyte balances)

In short, sodium doesn’t just “hold” water — it actively directs where water goes in the body by influencing osmotic pressure across cell membranes.
