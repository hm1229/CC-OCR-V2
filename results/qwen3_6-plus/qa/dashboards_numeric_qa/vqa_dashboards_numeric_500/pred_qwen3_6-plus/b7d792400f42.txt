Based on the infographic “The Untapped Potential of Women in Nonprofits” (April 28, 2014), here’s what it says about men holding top-level management positions in **large nonprofits**:

- **71%** of respondents from large nonprofits said their organization’s **CEO is male**.
- **69%** of respondents from large nonprofits said their organization’s **board was ‘predominantly male’**.
- **44%** of respondents from large nonprofits said their organization **favors males over equally qualified females for top positions**.

Additionally, while women make up **66%** of all nonprofit staff overall, leadership roles — especially at the CEO and board levels in large nonprofits — are still predominantly held by men.

---

### So, to directly answer your question:
> **What amount of top-level management is owned by men?**

While the infographic doesn’t use the word “owned,” if you mean “held” or “occupied,” then:

✅ **71% of CEOs** in large nonprofits are male.  
✅ **69% of boards** in large nonprofits are predominantly male.  
✅ **44% of organizations** favor men over equally qualified women for top positions.

These figures indicate that **men hold a significant majority of top-level leadership roles** in large nonprofits, despite women making up the majority of the overall nonprofit workforce.

---

*Note: The survey defines “large nonprofits” as organizations with annual revenue of $25 million or more.*

Source: Harris Poll for NYU SCPS & The Chronicle of Philanthropy, April 3–10, 2014.
