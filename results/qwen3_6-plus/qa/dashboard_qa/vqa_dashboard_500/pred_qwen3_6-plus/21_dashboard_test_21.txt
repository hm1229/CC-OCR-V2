As of July 2019, Texas’s **cumulative oil production** since January 2000 is **14.1 billion barrels**.

This figure is explicitly listed in the infographic under the “Texas” section:

> **Cumulative Production**: 14.1 billion barrels

---

### Context:
- This cumulative total represents all oil produced in Texas from **January 2000 through July 2019**.
- Texas alone accounted for more than 40% of U.S. national oil production by July 2019 (5,021,000 barrels/day out of ~12+ million barrels/day nationally).
- The six states highlighted (Texas, Alaska, California, North Dakota, Oklahoma, New Mexico) together produced 78% of U.S. oil in July 2019 and had cumulatively produced 73% of the nation’s oil since January 2000 — with Texas being the largest contributor by far.

So, the answer is:  
> **14.1 billion barrels**

*(Source: Infographic “United States of Oil” by Jeffrey Winters, Mechanical Engineering magazine)*
