The three basic approaches for organizing a network (as explained in the "SEARCH ENGINE BASICS" section of the infographic) are:  

1. **Crawling**: Search engines follow links to browse the web and discover pages.  
2. **Sorting**: Results are sorted by a variety of factors (e.g., relevance, recency, etc.).  
3. **Indexing**: Sorted pages are stored in an index, allowing search engines to quickly match queries with results.
