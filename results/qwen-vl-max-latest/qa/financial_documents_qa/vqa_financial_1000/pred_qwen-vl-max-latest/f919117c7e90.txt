This is a **Discipline Review Letter** issued by the **U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)**, specifically from the **Department of Health & Human Services, Public Health Service, Food and Drug Administration**, located in Rockville, MD.

The letter is addressed to **Takeda Global Research & Development Center, Inc.**, and it pertains to a **supplemental New Drug Application (NDA)** for **Actos® (pioglitazone HCl) tablets** in various dosages (15 mg, 30 mg, and 45 mg). The document is dated **February 19, 2007**, and references prior submissions and proposed labeling revisions.

### Key Characteristics:
- **Type of Letter**: Discipline Review Letter
- **Purpose**: To provide feedback on the clinical section of Takeda’s response to proposed labeling changes.
- **Focus**: Evaluation of the **PROactive trial** data, particularly regarding the **primary endpoint** and its implications for labeling.
- **Regulatory Context**: Part of the FDA's review process under **Section 505(b)** of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act.

### Summary:
This is an official regulatory communication from the FDA to a pharmaceutical company, outlining concerns about the clinical trial data and how it should be presented in the drug’s labeling—specifically, the rationale for moving certain information from the Clinical Studies section to the Adverse Reactions section due to the failure of the primary endpoint in the PROactive trial.
