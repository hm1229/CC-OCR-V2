According to the **Community Sport and Active Recreation COVID-19 Factsheet** from the Department of Local Government, Sport and Cultural Industries (Western Australia), for social distancing during outdoor activities:

> **“...ensuring 4 square metres of space per person and maintaining a 1.5 metre distance from others.”**

---

### So, to answer your question directly:

✅ **Area between persons**:  
→ **4 square metres per person** (this is the *space allocation* rule)  
→ **1.5 metres physical distance** from others (this is the *minimum separation* rule)

These are two complementary measures:
- The **4 square metres per person** ensures adequate personal space in group settings (e.g., bootcamps).
- The **1.5 metre distance** is the minimum physical gap that must be maintained between individuals at all times.

---

### Additional Notes:
- This applies to **outdoor activities** (including bootcamps) in groups of **no more than 10 people total**, including instructors.
- For **indoor yoga/Pilates in private residences**, the same **4 square metres per person** rule applies, plus good hygiene and no sharing of equipment.
- **Social distancing must remain** even when spectators are allowed (e.g., parent/carer).

---

📌 **In summary**:  
For social distancing, ensure **at least 1.5 metres between people** AND allocate **4 square metres of space per person** in group activities.
