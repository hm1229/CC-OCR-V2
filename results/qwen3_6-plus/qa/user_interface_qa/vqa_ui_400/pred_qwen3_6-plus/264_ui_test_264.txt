Based on the provided infographic from the UK Department of Health & Social Care, **antibody testing** (which answers the question “Have I had it?”) can be done in two ways:

1. **Laboratory test**  
   - A blood sample is taken by a medical professional (e.g., via venipuncture from the arm).
   - This test is currently being offered to NHS and social care staff, NHS hospital inpatients, and care home residents based on clinician’s advice.

2. **Home test**  
   - A ‘finger-prick’ blood sample is taken at home by the individual.
   - These tests are *not currently accurate enough* and are *not being offered* by the Government at present — but may be available in the future once more accurate versions are developed.

---

✅ So, the two ways antibody testing can be done are:  
> **Laboratory test (blood draw by professional)** and **Home test (finger-prick self-test)**

*(Note: The infographic clarifies that while both methods exist conceptually, only the laboratory test is currently in use for specific groups; home tests are not yet approved or distributed.)*
