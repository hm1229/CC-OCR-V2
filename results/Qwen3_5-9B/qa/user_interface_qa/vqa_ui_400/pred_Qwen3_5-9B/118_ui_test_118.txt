The disease that rots the potato and destroys the plant is **potato blight**.

As shown in the infographic, this blight was first announced in Ireland in **September 1845**, and by **1846**, it had destroyed over 80% of the potato crop — the staple food for many Irish people. The blight is caused by a water mold called *Phytophthora infestans*, which attacks the leaves, stems, and tubers of the potato plant, leading to rapid rot and total crop failure.

This devastation triggered the **Great Irish Famine (1845–1852)**, as millions depended on potatoes for survival.

> 🥔 *“The potato blight is a disease that rots the potato & destroys the plant.”* — as stated in the infographic.

---

**Bonus context from the image:**
- The blight spread rapidly due to warm, wet weather.
- It led to mass starvation, disease (like cholera and typhus), and emigration — known as the **Irish Diaspora**.
- British policies, including the Corn Laws and lack of relief, worsened the crisis.

So, while “potato blight” is the biological cause, the human tragedy was amplified by political and economic factors.
