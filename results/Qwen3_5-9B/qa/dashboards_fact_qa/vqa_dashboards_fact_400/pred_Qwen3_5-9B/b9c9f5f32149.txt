Based on the provided infographic from the Department of Health & Social Care, the two main types of coronavirus (COVID-19) tests are:

1.  **Swab test** – This answers the question: **“Do I have it?”**
    *   It involves taking a sample from the throat and nose.
    *   It can be done via a **Laboratory test** (by a medical professional) or a **Home test** (finger-prick test).
    *   It indicates whether a person currently has the virus and needs to isolate, return to work, or be re-tested.

2.  **Antibody test** – This answers the question: **“Have I had it?”**
    *   It is a blood test taken by a medical professional.
    *   It tells you whether you’ve been infected in the past, but **not** about your level of protection or immunity.
    *   At the time of the infographic, these tests were not yet accurate enough for public use and were being offered only to NHS/social care staff and hospital/care home residents based on clinician advice.

> ⚠️ Note: The infographic also mentions that home antibody tests were planned for future release once more accurate versions were available — but they were not yet offered to the general public at the time.

So, while there are subtypes (lab vs. home), the two fundamental categories are:
- **Swab test** (detects current infection)
- **Antibody test** (detects past infection)

These serve different purposes and are used for different public health decisions.
