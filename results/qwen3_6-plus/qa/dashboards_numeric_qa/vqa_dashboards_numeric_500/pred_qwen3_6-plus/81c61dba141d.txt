Actually, **Australia has 6 states and 2 territories** — making a total of **8 major administrative divisions**, as shown in your image.

The **6 states** are:
- New South Wales (NSW)
- Victoria (VIC)
- Queensland (QLD)
- Western Australia (WA)
- South Australia (SA)
- Tasmania (TAS)

The **2 mainland/territory regions** shown are:
- Northern Territory (NT)
- Australian Capital Territory (ACT)

> 💡 Note: While NT and ACT are not “states,” they are self-governing territories with similar powers to states in many areas. The image includes all 8 for completeness under “Living in Australia.”

So, if you’re asking strictly about **states**, the answer is **6**.

But if you’re referring to the **total number of major jurisdictions** depicted (as in the infographic), it’s **8**.

✅ Final Answer:  
**6 states** — but the image shows **8 regions** (6 states + 2 territories).
