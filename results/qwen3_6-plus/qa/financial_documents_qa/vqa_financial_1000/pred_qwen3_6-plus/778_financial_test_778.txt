Looking at the calendar for **December 2006**:

- The days of the week are labeled across the top: **Sun, Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat**
- December 1, 2006 falls on a **Friday** (as shown in the first row under “Fri”)
- Counting forward:
  - Dec 1 → Fri
  - Dec 2 → Sat
  - Dec 3 → Sun
  - ...
  - Dec 31 is the last day of the month.

Let’s count the weeks:

- Week 1: Dec 1 (Fri) – Dec 2 (Sat)
- Week 2: Dec 3 (Sun) – Dec 9 (Sat)
- Week 3: Dec 10 (Sun) – Dec 16 (Sat)
- Week 4: Dec 17 (Sun) – Dec 23 (Sat)
- Week 5: Dec 24 (Sun) – Dec 30 (Sat)
- **Dec 31** → next day after Dec 30 → **Sunday**

Also, visually in the calendar grid:

- The last row has only one entry: **31**, and it is under the **“Sun”** column.

✅ Therefore, **December 31, 2006 was a Sunday**.
