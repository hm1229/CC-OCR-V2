Ban Ki-moon's initiatives under the "Education First" initiative include:

1. **Put Every Child in School**: 
   - Addressing the issue of 61 million primary-aged children being out of school.
   - Emphasizing the need to eliminate cost barriers and identify and help children who need nutritional, health, and social support.
   - Highlighting the gender gap in education and the need to build more schools in conflict-affected areas.

2. **Improve the Quality of Learning**:
   - Addressing the issue of 250 million children lacking basic literacy and numeracy skills.
   - The need for 2 million more well-trained teachers and improved traditional and digital learning materials in students' home languages.
   - Aiming for a 45% increase in early childhood education programs.

3. **Foster Global Citizenship**:
   - Promoting peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity.
   - Cultivating a sense of community, active participation, and environmental stewardship.
   - Training teachers to use innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

4. **Close the Financial Gap**:
   - Calling for increased global investment in education, aiming for at least 5-6% of countries' national GDP to be invested in education.
   - Ensuring effective and accountable use of resources aligned with national education plans.

These initiatives aim to reduce poverty and hunger, end wasted potential, and build stronger and better societies for all.
