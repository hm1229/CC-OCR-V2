Mr. William Lucas (Horris) is always insensi- bly pushing, Miss Sheila Allen his wife, always palely appealing; Mr. James Howlett, the editor, always comically abashed by the events, and Mr. Albrey Richards, the father-in-law, always comically grotesque; they were not asked to modulate from their set woods but played with proper efficiency and, in the case of Mr. Richards, with lavish and suitably gaudy colour.
