Based on the infographic, **prescription drugs** (specifically opioids like prescription painkillers) lead to the most overdose deaths in the U.S.

Here’s the breakdown from the “Prescription Pills Are Compounding the Problem” section:

- **Prescription drugs**: 25,000+ overdose deaths
- **Cocaine**: ~5,500 overdose deaths
- **Heroin**: ~11,000 overdose deaths

Additionally, the infographic notes a **200% increase in overdose deaths involving opioids** (e.g., prescription painkillers or heroin) between 2000–2014.

It also states that in 2014, U.S. drug overdose deaths hit an all-time high, and that **47,055 people died** from drug overdoses that year — with prescription opioids being the largest contributor.

> 💡 *Note: While heroin deaths are significant (~11,000), prescription opioid deaths (~25,000+) are more than double that number.*

So, according to this data, **prescription opioids** are the leading cause of drug-related overdose deaths in the U.S.
