Based on the information provided in the infographic, the **fatality rate** is calculated using a formula from the **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)**.

The footnote at the bottom of the image explains:

> *“The fatality rate is per 100,000 full-time workers or equivalent, based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics’ formula.”*

It further clarifies the formula:

> *“The formula is the number of fatal injuries divided by number of hours worked by all employees during the calendar year, then multiplied by 200,000,000.”*

---

### Breakdown of the Formula:

**Fatality Rate = (Number of Fatal Injuries / Total Hours Worked by All Employees) × 200,000,000**

---

### Why 200,000,000?

This multiplier converts the rate to be **per 100,000 full-time equivalent workers**, assuming:

- A full-time worker works **40 hours per week**
- For **50 weeks per year** → 40 × 50 = **2,000 hours per year**
- So, 100,000 workers × 2,000 hours = **200,000,000 total hours**

Thus, multiplying by 200,000,000 standardizes the rate to reflect how many fatalities would occur per 100,000 full-time workers over a year.

---

### Example:

If an industry had 10 fatal injuries and 50,000,000 total hours worked in a year:

Fatality Rate = (10 / 50,000,000) × 200,000,000 = **40.0**

This means there were 40 fatalities per 100,000 full-time equivalent workers.

---

✅ So, in summary:  
**Fatality Rate = (Fatalities ÷ Total Hours Worked) × 200,000,000**  
→ This gives the number of deaths per 100,000 full-time equivalent workers annually.
