Women-owned firms outperformed male-owned firms in job creation by **1.24 million jobs** over the eight-year period from 2007 to 2015.

In terms of percentage growth:
- Female-owned firms saw an **18.39% growth** in employment (from 7.5 million to 8.9 million employees).
- Male-owned firms saw only a **0.37% growth** in employment (from 41.05 million to 41.2 million employees).

So, women-owned firms beat male-owned firms in job creation by a significant margin—**18.39% vs. 0.37%**, which is a difference of **18.02 percentage points** in growth rate.
