This is a **Discipline Review Letter** from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), part of the Department of Health & Human Services.

It’s addressed to Mary Jo Pritza, MPH, PharmD, Manager of Regulatory Affairs at Takeda Global Research & Development Center, Inc., regarding their supplemental New Drug Application (NDA) for Actos® (pioglitazone HCl) tablets.

The letter:

- References prior submissions and proposed labeling revisions.
- States that the FDA’s review of the clinical section of Takeda’s response is complete.
- Outlines specific concerns — particularly about the PROactive trial — including:
  - That it was a failed trial (primary endpoint not met).
  - Statistical issues with secondary endpoints.
  - Concerns that a proposed “main” secondary endpoint was added late in the study, giving it the appearance of a *post hoc* analysis rather than a prospectively defined one.

The tone is formal and regulatory, indicating the FDA is pushing back on certain aspects of Takeda’s proposed labeling changes based on clinical data interpretation.

Additionally, the document includes:
- A “Received” stamp from the Regulatory Affairs Department dated **February 19, 2007**.
- Confidentiality notice: “Confidential – Subject to Protective Order.”
- Production metadata indicating it was produced in MDL on 9/25/12 (likely for legal proceedings).

In summary:  
> This is an official FDA Discipline Review Letter challenging Takeda’s proposed labeling changes for Actos based on statistical and methodological concerns related to the PROactive clinical trial.
