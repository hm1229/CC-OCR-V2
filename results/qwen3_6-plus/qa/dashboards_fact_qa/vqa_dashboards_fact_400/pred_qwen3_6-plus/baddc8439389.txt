The biscuits used in WWI soldier rations were **Anzac biscuits**.

According to the infographic:

> “Anzac biscuits are closely associated with the Australian and New Zealand Army Corps (ANZAC), established in WWI.  
> **Bread substitute** — The original biscuits used in WWI soldier rations were hard and savoury.”

It also notes that the first known Anzac biscuit recipe was published in 1917 in *‘The War Chest Cookery Book’*, and that eggs were scarce during the war, so syrup was used as a binding agent — making them “eggless.”

So, while modern Anzac biscuits are sweet and made with oats, coconut, syrup, and sugar, their wartime predecessors were harder, savoury versions designed for long shelf life and ease of transport — serving as a bread substitute for soldiers.

✅ **Answer: Anzac biscuits** (specifically, the hard, savoury original version used in WWI rations).
