During **Operation Phantom Fury** (also known as the **Second Battle of Fallujah**) in 2004, the **Coalition forces** had significantly greater strength compared to the insurgents.

### Breakdown of Strength:

- **Insurgents:**
  - **Men:** Approximately **4,000**
  - Comprised mainly of Al-Qaeda-linked fighters and local insurgents under leaders like **Al-Janabi** and **Hadid**

- **Coalition Forces:**
  - **Men:** Around **10,500 U.S. troops**, **2,000 Iraqi soldiers**, and **850 British troops** — totaling about **13,350** combat personnel
  - Commanders: **U.S. General Mattis**, **U.S. General Natonaski**, and **U.K. General Cowan**
  - Supported by **airpower, armored vehicles, artillery, and extensive intelligence and logistical support**

### Conclusion:
The **Coalition** had a **clear numerical and technological advantage** over the insurgents. Their superior firepower, coordination, air support, and mobility allowed them to conduct a sustained urban assault, bomb the city for weeks, and eventually clear it of insurgents. The operation resulted in heavy insurgent casualties and the evacuation of most civilians.

Thus, **the Coalition had far more strength** during Operation Phantom Fury.
