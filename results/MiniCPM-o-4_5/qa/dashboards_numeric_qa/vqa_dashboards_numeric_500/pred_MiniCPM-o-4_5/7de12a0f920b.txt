Looking at the section "WORKING REMOTELY BEFORE, DURING AND AFTER THE PANDEMIC", it states:

"22.5% of businesses expect that 10% or more of their workforce will continue to work remotely once the pandemic is over."

The other percentages (16.6% and 32.6%) refer to the proportion of businesses reporting remote work *before* and *on May 29, 2020* respectively.

Therefore, the correct percentage for workers continuing to work remotely after the pandemic is 22.5%.

Answer: 22.5%
