Based on the infographic, the candidate for president is chosen through a multi-step process:

1.  **Caucuses and State Primaries:** The process begins at the state level. Voters in each state participate in either caucuses or primary elections to select their party's nominees for president.
2.  **Party Delegates:** After the primaries/caucuses, party delegates from each state are selected. These delegates represent the preferences of the voters in their state.
3.  **National Party Conventions:** The party delegates then travel to their respective party's National Party Convention.
4.  **Official Selection:** At the National Party Convention, the delegates officially vote to choose the party's candidate for president.

So, while the initial popular vote happens in individual states, the **official choice of the party's presidential candidate is made at the National Party Convention** by the delegates who were selected during the state primaries and caucuses.
