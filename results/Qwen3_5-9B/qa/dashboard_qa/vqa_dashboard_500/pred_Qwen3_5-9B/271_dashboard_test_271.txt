A 60-watt bulb can use up to **5 gallons of water** for every hour it is left on.

This “hidden water” refers to the water used in the production and supply chain of electricity — including mining, processing, and generating the power that runs the bulb. So even though the bulb itself doesn’t consume water directly, its operation indirectly uses water through the energy grid.

✅ **Answer: 5 gallons per hour**
