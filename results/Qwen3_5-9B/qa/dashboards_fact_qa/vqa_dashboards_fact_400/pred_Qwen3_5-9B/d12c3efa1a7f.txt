To find the **food price change of beef and bread, taken together**, we need to interpret what “taken together” means in this context.

Looking at the infographic under **“FOOD PRICE CHANGES SINCE 2006”**, we see:

- **BEEF**: **29.4%**
- **BREAD**: **40.9%**

These are individual percentage changes. If the question is asking for the **combined or average** price change (since they’re different items), the most reasonable interpretation is to take the **simple average** of the two percentages — unless specified otherwise (e.g., weighted by quantity sold, which we don’t have).

---

### Step-by-step:

Add the two percentages:

> 29.4% + 40.9% = **70.3%**

Divide by 2 to get the average:

> 70.3% ÷ 2 = **35.15%**

---

### ✅ Final Answer:
**35.15%**

*(This represents the average food price change for beef and bread combined since 2006.)*
