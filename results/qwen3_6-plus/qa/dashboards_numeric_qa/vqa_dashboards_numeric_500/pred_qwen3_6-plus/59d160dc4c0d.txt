Based on the infographic, the **candidate for president is chosen at the National Party Conventions**.

Here’s how the process works according to the image:

1.  **Caucuses and State Primaries**: Voters in each state participate in caucuses or primaries to express their preference for a presidential nominee.
2.  **Delegates**: Based on the results of these caucuses and primaries, each state sends **party delegates** to the National Party Conventions.
3.  **National Party Conventions**: At these conventions, the delegates officially vote to choose their party’s candidate for president.

The infographic visually represents this with a winding road showing:
- Electorate → Caucuses/State Primaries → Party Delegates → Candidate for President

So, while voters influence the outcome through primaries and caucuses, the formal selection of the candidate happens at the **National Party Conventions** by the delegates.

---

*Note: The infographic also mentions that the weight of each state in this process depends on its number of “Electors at Large” (which equals its number of congressional districts + 2 senators), but this relates more to the general election Electoral College, not the primary nomination process.*
