To determine how a presidential candidate is chosen, refer to the **"THE SYSTEM"** section of the infographic:  

1. **Primary/Caucus Stage**: Voters in each state participate in *caucuses* (local meetings) or *state primaries* to select their party’s nominee for president.  
2. **National Convention Stage**: Delegates from each state (who were elected in the primary/caucus) then attend the **National Party Convention**. At these conventions, delegates formally vote to select the party’s official presidential candidate.  

After this, the candidate must win enough *Electoral College votes* (at least 270) to become president. The infographic emphasizes that the process relies on state-level delegate voting at national conventions, rather than a direct popular vote for president.
