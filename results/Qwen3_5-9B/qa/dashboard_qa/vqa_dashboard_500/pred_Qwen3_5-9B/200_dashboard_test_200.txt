The area between the wickets in cricket is called the **pitch**.

More specifically, it’s the central rectangular strip of the cricket field where the main action takes place — including the bowler’s run-up, the batsman’s stance, and the ball’s trajectory. The pitch is 22 yards (20.12 meters) long and is marked by two sets of wickets at either end.

In the infographic you shared, the green rectangular area with the stumps (wickets) at each end is visually representing this **pitch**.

> 🏏 *Fun fact: The pitch is often referred to as “the strip” by players and commentators.*
