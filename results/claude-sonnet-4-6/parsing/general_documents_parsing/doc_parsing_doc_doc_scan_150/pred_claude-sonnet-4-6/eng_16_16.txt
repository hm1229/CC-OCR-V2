$$_b^N t = A_t \log \frac{b}{b - \frac{1}{10}}.$$

Also $R$ being the reduction, (iii.) may be written

$$Z = A_t \log \frac{\beta + R}{\beta} \ .$$

Combining these, we get

$$\log \left(1 + \frac{R}{\beta}\right) = \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \log \frac{10\,b}{10\,b - 1} \, ;$$

hence, \quad $1 + \dfrac{R}{\beta} = \left(\dfrac{10\,b}{10\,b - 1}\right)^{\dfrac{Z}{_b^N t}} = \left(1 - \dfrac{1}{10\,b}\right)^{-\dfrac{Z}{_b^N t}}$

$$= 1 + \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \frac{1}{10\,b} + \frac{1}{1.2} \cdot \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \overline{\frac{Z}{_b^N t} + 1} \cdot \overline{\frac{1}{10\,b}}^{\,2} + \ldots$$

by the binomial theorem.

$$\therefore R = \beta \left( \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \frac{1}{10\,b} + \frac{1}{1.2} \cdot \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \overline{\frac{Z}{_b^N t} + 1} \cdot \overline{\frac{1}{10\,b}}^{\,2} + \ldots \right) \tag{iv.}$$

Formula (i.) is deduced from (iv.), by neglecting all terms beyond
the first; and making $b = 30$ inches, if used with Table XVI.; but,
if used with Table XIX.$'$, $b$ may be any reading within the range of
the table, and $_b^N t$ the \textit{corresponding} number from the table.

Although (i.) is sufficiently accurate for small heights, it is evident,
on comparing it with the full formula (iv.), that it becomes more and
more inaccurate as the height increases.

If, in (i.), the reduced height $B$, were substituted for the observed
height $\beta$, the error would be relatively less; for Laplace's formula
may also be expanded in the form

$$R = B \left( \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \frac{1}{10\,b} - \frac{1}{1.2} \cdot \frac{Z}{_b^N t} \cdot \overline{\frac{Z}{_b^N} - 1} \cdot \overline{\frac{1}{10\,b}}^{\,2} + \ldots \right) \tag{v.}$$
