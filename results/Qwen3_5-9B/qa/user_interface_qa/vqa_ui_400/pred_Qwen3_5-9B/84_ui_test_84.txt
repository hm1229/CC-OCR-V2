### What about cerebrospinal fluid?

Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) is a clear fluid that surrounds the brain and spinal cord. It cushions the brain, removes waste, and provides nutrients.

The human body contains approximately **150 mL** of CSF, which is about **0.015 liters** or **15 grams** (since density ≈ 1 g/mL).

Total body water is roughly **42 liters** (in an average adult), so:

> **CSF as % of total body water** = (150 mL / 42,000 mL) × 100 ≈ **0.36%**

That’s far less than any of the numbers shown.

Alternatively, if you’re asking what percentage of the *brain* is cerebrospinal fluid — that doesn’t make sense either, because CSF is *outside* the brain tissue, filling the ventricles and subarachnoid space. The brain itself is mostly water (~75–80%), but not CSF.
