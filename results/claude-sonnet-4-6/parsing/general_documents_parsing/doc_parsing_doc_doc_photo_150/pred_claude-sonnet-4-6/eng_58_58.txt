In Exercises 33--40, find an equation of the tangent line to the graph
of $y = f(x)$ at the given $x$. Do not apply formula (6), but proceed
as we did in Example 4.

\textbf{33.} $f(x) = x^3, x = -2$ \hspace{2cm} \textbf{34.} $f(x) = x^2, x = -\frac{1}{2}$

\textbf{35.} $f(x) = 3x + 1, x = 4$ \hspace{1.7cm} \textbf{36.} $f(x) = 5, x = -2$

\textbf{37.} $f(x) = \sqrt{x},\ x = \frac{1}{9}$ \hspace{2cm} \textbf{38.} $f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x},\ x = .01$

\textbf{39.} $f(x) = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}},\ x = 1$ \hspace{2cm} \textbf{40.} $f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x^3},\ x = 3$

\textbf{41.} The point--slope form of the equation of the tangent line to
the graph of $y = x^4$ at the point $(1, 1)$ is $y - 1 = 4(x - 1)$.
Explain how this equation follows from formula (6).

\textbf{42.} The tangent to the graph of $y = \frac{1}{x}$ at the point $P = \left(a, \frac{1}{a}\right)$,
where $a > 0$, is perpendicular to the line $y = 4x + 1$. Find $P$.

\textbf{43.} The line $y = 2x + b$ is tangent to the graph of $y = \sqrt{x}$ at
the point $P = (a,\ \sqrt{a})$. Find $P$ and determine $b$.

\textbf{44.} The line $y = ax + b$ is tangent to the graph of $y = x^3$ at the
point $P = (-3, -27)$. Find $a$ and $b$.

\textbf{45. (a)} Find the point on the curve $y = \sqrt{x}$ where the tangent
line is parallel to the line $y = \dfrac{x}{8}$.

\hspace{0.6cm}\textbf{(b)} On the same axes, plot the curve $y = \sqrt{x}$, the line
$y = \dfrac{x}{8}$, and the tangent line to $y = \sqrt{x}$ that is parallel
to $y = \dfrac{x}{8}$.

\textbf{46.} There are two points on the graph of $y = x^3$ where the tan-
gent lines are parallel to $y = x$. Find these points.

\textbf{47.} Is there any point on the graph of $y = x^3$ where the tangent
line is perpendicular to $y = x$? Justify your answer.

\textbf{48.} The graph of $y = f(x)$ goes through the point $(2, 3)$ and the
equation of the tangent line at that point is $y = -2x + 7$.
Find $f(2)$ and $f'(2)$.
