Lazlo Toth was arrested for **industrial espionage**.

According to the article, he was detained in Yugoslavia on August 1 (the year prior to the article’s publication — so likely August 1, 1975) after allegedly taking photographs of a beet-sugar plant in Vrbas, a suburb of Belgrade. He was the Director of Research and Development at the Great Western Sugar Co. in Loveland, Colorado, and was charged with industrial espionage following a closed-door trial. He was sentenced to seven years in prison before being pardoned by the Yugoslav collective presidency in July 1976.

The article notes that his exact release date was still unknown at the time of publication (July 16, 1976), though U.S. officials were pressing for it.
