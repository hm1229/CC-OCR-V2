Based on the provided infographic, **Ban Ki-moon’s “Education First” initiative** — launched during his tenure as UN Secretary-General — is built around **three core pillars**, each with specific goals and actions. Here's a breakdown of the initiatives:

---

## 🎯 **The Three Core Pillars of Education First**

### 1. **Put Every Child in School**
> *“When you ask parents what they want for their children—even in war zones and disaster areas—they seek the same thing: education.”*

**Key Facts & Goals:**
- **61 million primary-aged children** are out of school.
  - 32 million are girls.
  - Over 28 million live in conflict-affected poor countries.
  - Many are shut out due to poverty, lack of classrooms, or hunger.

**Actions Needed (“We Must”):**
- Eliminate cost barriers to attending and completing school.
- Identify and help children needing nutritional, health, and social support.
- Close the gender gap at all education levels.
- Build **4 million more classrooms**, especially in rural and conflict-affected areas.
- **Double or more** education’s percentage of humanitarian aid.

---

### 2. **Improve the Quality of Learning**
> *“250 million children cannot read, write or count well, even those with at least four years in school.”*

**Key Facts & Goals:**
- Millions finish school without basic literacy/numeracy.
- Education can lift people out of poverty: If all students in low-income countries acquired basic reading skills, **171 million could be lifted out of poverty** (equivalent to a 12% cut in world poverty).

**Actions Needed (“We Need”):**
- Recruit **2 million more well-trained teachers** with effective student performance measures.
- Improve traditional and digital learning materials in students’ home languages.
- Increase early-childhood education programs by **45%** to prepare children for primary school.
- Better match schooling with workforce skills and adult learning.

---

### 3. **Foster Global Citizenship**
> *“Education gives us a profound understanding that we are tied together as citizens of the global community...”*

**Key Facts & Goals:**
- The world faces global challenges requiring global solutions.
- Education must cultivate care for the world and others.

**Actions Needed (“It Is Important To”):**
- Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity across cultures through education.
- Cultivate a sense of community, active participation, and environmental stewardship.
- Train teachers to use innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

---

## 💰 **Cross-Cutting Priority: Close the Financial Gap**

> *“Investments in education are inadequate to maintain gains made in the past decade or meet ambitious goals.”*

**Actions Needed (“We Must”):**
- Generate additional and sufficient funding through sustained global advocacy.
- Increase global investment in poor countries’ school systems by **$24 billion per year**.
- Invest **at least 5–6% of countries’ national GDP** in education.
- Ensure effective and accountable use of resources aligned with national education plans.

---

## 🌍 Overall Vision & Quotes from Ban Ki-moon

- *“When we put Education First, we can reduce poverty and hunger, end wasted potential—and look forward to stronger and better societies for all.”*
- *“Education is the single best investment countries can make towards building prosperous, healthy and equitable societies.”*

✅ In summary, Ban Ki-moon’s **Education First** initiative is a comprehensive global campaign focused on:
1. **Access** (getting every child into school),
2. **Quality** (ensuring they learn meaningful skills),
3. **Global Citizenship** (preparing them to contribute to a peaceful, sustainable world),
4. **Financing** (closing the funding gap to make it all possible).

This initiative reflects his belief that **education is foundational to achieving all other development goals** — from poverty reduction to peacebuilding.
