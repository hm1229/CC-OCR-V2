During the Battle of Blore Heath in 1459, the Lancastrians had more strength on paper with a force of 8,000 to 14,000 men, compared to the Yorkists' 3,000 to 5,000 men. However, the Yorkists were able to achieve a tactical victory by feigning defeat, causing confusion, and then counterattacking. The Lancastrians suffered significant casualties, including the death of their commander, Baron Audley.
