To determine which part of America is severely affected by the drought for corn production, we analyze the **U.S. corn producing areas** map and the drought intensity map:  

1. **Corn Production Regions**: The U.S. “Corn Belt” (major corn - growing areas) is in the **Mid - East/Central region** (states like Illinois, Indiana, Missouri, Iowa, Kansas, Ohio). These are labeled as “Major growing area” (dark green) on the corn - producing map.  

2. **Drought Impact on Corn Belt**: On the large drought intensity map, these Mid - East/Central states (e.g., IL, IN, MO, KS) show **D3 (Extreme) or D2 (Severe) drought** (orange/yellow shades). These are severe drought categories.  

3. **Western Region**: Western states (e.g., CA, NV, UT, AZ) are not major corn - producing areas (they appear as “Drought area” or “Minor growing area” on the corn map). While they experience drought (D2), the *corn production impact* in the West is far less critical because these states aren’t primary corn - growing regions.  


Thus, the **eastern part (specifically the Mid - East/Corn Belt region)** is severely affected by the drought for corn production.
