## Western Part of America

Based on the infographic, the **western part of America** is more severely affected by the drought in terms of corn production.

The map showing **U.S. corn producing areas** indicates that:

- The **major growing areas** (dark green) are concentrated in the **central to eastern regions** (Midwest/Corn Belt)
- However, the **drought area** (shown in tan/beige) heavily overlaps with the **western and central corn-producing regions**
- States like **CO, KS, NE, OK, TX, NM** in the western/central belt show significant drought impact on corn-producing areas

The drought originated in the **Midwest**, ravaging fields across the central and western corn-producing states, which is why corn production dropped from an expected **14 billion bushels** to only **11 billion bushels** — a reduction of approximately **3 billion bushels**.
