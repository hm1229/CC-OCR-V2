# Education First - Ban Ki-moon's Initiatives

This infographic outlines **The United Nations Secretary-General's initiative** called **"Education First"** with three main pillars:

## Three Core Initiatives:

1. **Put Every Child in School**
2. **Improve the Quality of Learning**
3. **Foster Global Citizenship**

---

## 1. PUT EVERY CHILD IN SCHOOL

**Key Facts:**
- **61 million** primary-aged children are out of school
- **32 million** of these are girls
- Over **28 million** are in conflict-affected poor countries
- Most are shut out because of poverty, but others lack a classroom or go to school hungry

**We Must:**
- Eliminate cost barriers to attending and completing school
- Identify and help children who need nutritional, health, and social support
- Close the gender gap at all education levels
- Reach **4 million** more classrooms, especially in rural and conflict-affected areas
- **Double** or more education's percentage of humanitarian aid

---

## 2. IMPROVE THE QUALITY OF LEARNING

**Key Facts:**
- **250 million** children cannot read, write, or count well, even those with at least four years in school

**We Need:**
- **2 million** more well-trained teachers with effective student performance measures
- Improved traditional and digital learning materials in students' home languages
- A **45% increase** in early-childhood education programs to prepare children for primary school
- A better match between schooling and needed workforce skills, and more adult learning

**Quote:** *"Education lifts people out of poverty. If all students in low-income countries acquire basic reading skills, 171 million people could be lifted out of poverty, equivalent to a 12 percent cut in world poverty."*

---

## 3. FOSTER GLOBAL CITIZENSHIP

**It is Important to:**
- Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity across cultures through education
- Cultivate a sense of community, active participation, and environmental stewardship
- Train teachers to use innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching and learning methods

**Quote:** *"Education gives us a profound understanding that we are tied together as citizens of the global community, and that our challenges are interconnected."*

---

## CLOSE THE FINANCIAL GAP

**We Must:**
- Generate additional and sufficient funding for education through sustained global advocacy efforts
- Increase global investment in poor countries' school systems by **$24 billion per year**
- Invest at least **5-6%** of countries' national GDP in education
- Ensure effective and accountable use of resources aligned with national education plans

**Quote:** *"Education is the single best investment countries can make towards building prosperous, healthy and equitable societies."*
