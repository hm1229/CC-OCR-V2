Actually, **Helen of Sparta did not die in 1184 BC** — that date is likely a misinterpretation or error in the infographic.

Let’s clarify:

### Helen of Sparta (also known as Helen of Troy)
- **Lived**: Traditionally placed around **12th century BC**, during the time of the Trojan War.
- The infographic says “**1194–1184 BC**” — this likely refers to the **traditional dates of the Trojan War** (starting 1194 BC, ending 1184 BC with the fall of Troy), **not Helen’s lifespan**.
- There is **no historical record of Helen’s death date**. She is a figure from Greek mythology and epic poetry (especially Homer’s *Iliad* and *Odyssey*), so her life events are legendary, not historically documented.
- In myth, after the Trojan War, she returned to Sparta with her husband Menelaus and lived out her life there. Some later traditions say she was deified or lived peacefully; others suggest darker fates — but none are historically verifiable.

---

### So, to answer your question directly:
> **When was Helen of Sparta died?**

✅ **There is no known or recorded date of Helen of Sparta’s death.**  
The “1184 BC” shown in the infographic almost certainly refers to the **end of the Trojan War**, not her death.
