\section*{3 PRESENT CONTINUOUS}

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline
\textbf{Form} & am, is, or are + verb + ing \\
\hline
\textbf{Use} & actions happening now, at the moment of speaking. \\
\hline
\textbf{Signal words} & now, at the moment, still, Look! Listen!... \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\subsection*{EXERCISE 1 Find the signal words for present continuous.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Which is a signal word for present continuous?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item last -- now -- every night
    \end{itemize}
    \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present continuous?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item now - already - still
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{4 PAST CONTINUOUS}

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline
\textbf{Form} & was or were + verb + ing \\
\hline
\textbf{Use} & For actions which were in progress in the past. \\
\hline
\textbf{Signal words} & when, while + long action in the past \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\subsection*{EXERCISE 1 Find the signal words for past continuous.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Which is a signal word for past continuous?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item while - by next week - tomorrow
    \end{itemize}
    \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for past continuous?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item next week -- when - while
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{5 PRESENT PERFECT}

\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline
\textbf{Form} & have or has + Past Participle \\
\hline
\textbf{Use} & For actions which started in the past and continued up to the present. \\
\hline
\textbf{Signal words} & just, already, yet, for, since, so far, recently, ever... \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

NB. We use Present Perfect Continuous (have/has+ been + verb +ing) to give more emphasis on the duration of the action. However, some verbs can't be used in the continuous form such as own, smell, need, belong, cost, agree, contain..., etc.

\subsection*{EXERCISE 1 Find the signal words for present perfect.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Which is a signal word for present perfect?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item still -- usually -- since
    \end{itemize}
    \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present perfect?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item since -- before -- for
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{EXERCISE 2 Put these verbs in present perfect.}

    \item They/ to do
    \item Jack / to publish
    \item He/ not, to have
    \item She/ to study
    \item Mike and Lina / to have
\end{enumerate}
