CASE STUDY: ACFB CANCER REHABILITATION
OF A NON-RECURRENT PATIENT

b. Patient Name: Belinda
Belinda is a 50 y/o female with NED (No Evidence of Disease) after treatment for stage
IIA left breast cancer. She was diagnosed in 2011 and was treated with lumpectomy and
sentinel node dissection, adjuvant chemotherapy, and adjuvant radiation therapy.
Belinda was diagnosed with left shoulder adhesive capsulitis and was referred to
physical therapy by her PCP. She was also referred to the ACFB Cancer Rehabilitation
Program by her oncologist due to concerns about deconditioning and weight gain.
Subjective/Assessment:
Belinda states she is still “getting over” treatment and all she wants to do is sleep. She
reports she has gained 20 pounds and is “embarrassed” to be seen in public. She
says she is “too tired” to exercise and has no energy to do anything. She says she
“wants to get back to normal” but doesn’t know where to start. She says she has
trouble sleeping and often wakes up feeling unrefreshed. She says she has no
motivation and “just doesn’t care anymore.” She says she has not returned to work
and spends most of the day watching TV. She says she has no appetite and often skips
meals. She says she has no interest in seeing friends or family and has stopped going
to church. She says she feels “hopeless” and “like a burden” to her husband. She
denies suicidal ideation but says she sometimes wishes she “wouldn’t wake up.”
