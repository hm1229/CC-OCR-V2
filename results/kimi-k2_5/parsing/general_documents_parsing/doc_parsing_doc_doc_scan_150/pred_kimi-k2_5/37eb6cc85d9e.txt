$\displaystyle SE_{\bar{x}} = \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$ & $\displaystyle \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(o-e)^2}{e}$ \\

\noindent\textbf{Definitions:}

\begin{itemize}
\item[] \textbf{Mode} = value that occurs most frequently in a data set
\item[] \textbf{Median} = middle value that separates the greater and lesser halves of a data set
\item[] \textbf{Mean} = sum of all data points divided by number of data points
\item[] \textbf{Range} = value obtained by subtracting the smallest observation (sample minimum) from the greatest (sample maximum)
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent\textbf{Key:}

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
$\bar{x}$ = sample mean & $o$ = observed results \\
$n$ = size of the sample & $e$ = expected results \\
$s$ = sample standard deviation (i.e., the sample-based & \\
\quad estimate of the standard deviation of the & \\
\quad population) & Degrees of freedom are equal to the number of \\
& \quad distinct possible outcomes minus one. \\
\end{tabular}
