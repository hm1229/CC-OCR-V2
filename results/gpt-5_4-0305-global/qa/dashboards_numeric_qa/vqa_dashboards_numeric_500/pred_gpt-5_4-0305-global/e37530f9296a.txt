From the note at the top/bottom of the infographic:

**Fatality rate = (number of fatal work injuries / number of workers) × 100,000**

So it’s expressed as **deaths per 100,000 full-time-equivalent workers per year**, using the **Bureau of Labor Statistics’ formula**.

Example:
- If an occupation has 10 deaths among 50,000 workers:
- \( (10 / 50{,}000) \times 100{,}000 = 20 \)

So the fatality rate would be **20**.
