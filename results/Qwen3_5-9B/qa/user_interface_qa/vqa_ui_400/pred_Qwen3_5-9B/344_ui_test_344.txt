During the **Battle of Zama (204 B.C.)**, **Carthage** had more infantry.

- **Romans**: 34,000 infantry  
- **Carthage**: 50,000 infantry

So, Carthage fielded **16,000 more infantry** than Rome.

Despite this numerical advantage in infantry, Carthage ultimately lost the battle due to Roman tactical superiority — particularly the use of Numidian cavalry and the disciplined Roman legions that outmaneuvered Hannibal’s forces.

✅ **Answer: Carthage had more infantry (50,000 vs. 34,000).**
