Among the top 5 causes of accidental deaths listed, motor vehicle accidents are the major reason, accounting for 42,000 annual deaths. This is higher than the deaths caused by poisoning (39,000 annual deaths) and significantly higher than the other causes: choking (2,500), fires (2,700), and falls (25,000). Distracted driving is highlighted as the number one offender in motor vehicle accidents, with young adults being the primary culprits due to their higher crash rate compared to other age groups.
