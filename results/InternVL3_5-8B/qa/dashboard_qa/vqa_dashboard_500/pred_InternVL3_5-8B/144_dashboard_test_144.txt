Halloween pumpkins are characterized by their large size, round shape, and vibrant orange color. These pumpkins are typically harvested in the fall and are a central element of Halloween decorations. They are often used for carving jack-o'-lanterns, which are illuminated from within and displayed on porches and around homes. The tradition of carving pumpkins into jack-o'-lanterns dates back to the early 19th century and has since become a beloved Halloween activity. Additionally, pumpkins are a significant agricultural product in the United States, with millions of pumpkins produced annually specifically for Halloween.
