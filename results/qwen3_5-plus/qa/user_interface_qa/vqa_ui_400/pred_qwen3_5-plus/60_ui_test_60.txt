Based on the infographic provided, Ban Ki-moon's initiative is titled **"Education First."**

The initiative is built around three main priorities, along with a section on funding:

**1. Put Every Child in School**
*   **Goal:** Address the 61 million primary-aged children currently out of school (32 million of whom are girls).
*   **Actions required:**
    *   Eliminate cost barriers to attending school.
    *   Identify and help children who need nutritional, health, and social support.
    *   Close the gender gap at all education levels.
    *   Build 4 million more classrooms, especially in rural and conflict-affected areas.
    *   Double (or more) the percentage of humanitarian aid dedicated to education.

**2. Improve the Quality of Learning**
*   **Goal:** Address the fact that 250 million children cannot read, write, or count well, even after four years of school.
*   **Actions required:**
    *   Recruit 2 million more well-trained teachers.
    *   Improve traditional and digital learning materials in students' home languages.
    *   Increase early-childhood education programs by 45%.
    *   Create a better match between schooling and needed workforce skills.

**3. Foster Global Citizenship**
*   **Goal:** Cultivate an active care for the world and those with whom we share it to solve global challenges.
*   **Actions required:**
    *   Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity.
    *   Cultivate a sense of community, active participation, and environmental stewardship.
    *   Train teachers to use innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

**4. Close the Financial Gap**
*   **Goal:** Secure adequate investment to maintain gains and meet the goals of Education First.
*   **Actions required:**
    *   Generate additional funding through global advocacy.
    *   Increase global investment in poor countries' school systems by **$24 billion per year**.
    *   Invest at least **5 to 6% of countries' national GDP** in education.
