MICHAEL SCOT

THE LIFE AND LEGEND OF

Michael Scot

The life and legend of Michael Scot, the “Wizard of the North,” have fascinated readers for centuries. This volume explores the historical figure behind the myths, tracing his career as a scholar, astrologer, and translator in the courts of medieval Europe. Drawing on a wide range of sources, it examines how Scot’s reputation developed over time and how later generations transformed him into a legendary magician.

The book also considers Scot’s intellectual milieu, including the transmission of Arabic science and philosophy into Latin Christendom, and his role in the cultural exchanges of the thirteenth century. By situating Scot within the broader context of medieval learning, the study sheds light on the interplay between history and legend in the making of one of Scotland’s most enduring figures.
