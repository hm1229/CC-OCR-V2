Based on the provided infographic from the UK Department of Health & Social Care, the two main types of Coronavirus (COVID-19) tests are:

1.  **Swab test** – This test answers the question: **“Do I have it?”**  
    It detects whether a person is *currently infected* with the virus.

2.  **Antibody test** – This test answers the question: **“Have I had it?”**  
    It detects whether a person has *previously been infected* by looking for antibodies in their blood.

---

The infographic further breaks down the **Antibody test** into two sub-types:
*   **Laboratory test**: Blood taken by a medical professional.
*   **Home test**: ‘Finger-prick’ test taken at home (noted as not currently accurate or available).

So, while there are three testing *methods* shown (swab, lab antibody, home antibody), the fundamental distinction made at the top level is between **Swab tests** (for current infection) and **Antibody tests** (for past infection).

✅ Therefore, the two types of coronavirus tests highlighted are:
> **Swab test** and **Antibody test**

*(Note: The “Laboratory test” and “Home test” are categories under Antibody testing, not separate primary types alongside Swab.)*
