WESTERN UNION TELEGRAM

The firm mentioned at the top of the page is **WESTERN UNION TELEGRAM**. This is prominently displayed in large, bold letters at the top of the telegram form. Western Union was a major American telecommunications company that provided telegram and money transfer services. The form also indicates that W.P. Marshall was the president of the company at this time. This particular telegram was sent on December 30, 1961, from Houston, Texas, and was addressed to Dr. Roger Adams at the Department of Chemistry, University of Illinois, Urbana, Illinois. The message was from Mark Edwin Andrews, informing Dr. Adams about his father's death and expressing appreciation for Dr. Adams' service as a trustee of the Welch Foundation.
