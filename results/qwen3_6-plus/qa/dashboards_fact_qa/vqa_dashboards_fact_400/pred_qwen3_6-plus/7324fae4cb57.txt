Looking at the infographic, we can find the relevant data in the top section:

> **21% INTERNET USERS WHO ARE EUROPEAN**

This means that **21% of all global internet users are European**.

Therefore, to find the percentage of internet users who are **not** Europeans, we subtract this from 100%:

> **100% - 21% = 79%**

---

✅ **Answer: 79%**

*(Note: The “12% CONNECTED WORLD POPULATION” refers to the fact that Europeans make up 12% of the world’s total population — not directly related to the share of global internet users.)*
