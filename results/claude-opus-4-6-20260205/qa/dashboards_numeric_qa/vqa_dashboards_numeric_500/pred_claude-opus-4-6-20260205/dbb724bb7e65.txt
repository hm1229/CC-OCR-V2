## Key Job Creation Comparison

Based on the infographic, female-owned firms showed **18.39% growth** in employment (from 7.5M to 8.9M employees between 2007-2015), compared to just **0.37% growth** for male-owned firms (from 41.05M to 41.2M employees in the same period).

This means **female-owned firms beat male-owned firms by 18.02 percentage points** in job creation growth rate (18.39% - 0.37% = 18.02%).

In absolute terms, female-owned firms created **1.4 million new jobs** (8.9M - 7.5M) compared to **0.15 million (150,000) new jobs** by male-owned firms (41.2M - 41.05M) during that eight-year period.

The infographic specifically states that female-owned firms created **1.24 million more jobs** than their male-owned counterparts during that eight-year period, even though men run more businesses.
