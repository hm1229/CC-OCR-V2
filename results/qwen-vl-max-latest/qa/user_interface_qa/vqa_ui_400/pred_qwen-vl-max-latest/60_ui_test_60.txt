The infographic highlights **Ban Ki-moon's "Education First"** initiative, which is the United Nations Secretary-General’s global campaign to prioritize education. The core initiatives and goals of this initiative are organized into three main pillars:

---

### **1. Put Every Child in School**
- **Goal:** Ensure universal access to quality primary education.
- **Key Facts:**
  - 61 million primary-aged children are out of school (32 million are girls).
  - Over 25 million are in conflict-affected areas.
  - Most drop out due to poverty, lack of classrooms, or hunger.

- **Actions Required:**
  - Eliminate cost barriers to attending and completing school.
  - Identify and help children who need nutritional, health, and social support.
  - Close the gender gap at all education levels.
  - Build **4 million more classrooms**, especially in rural and conflict-affected areas.
  - **Double or more** education’s percentage of humanitarian aid.

---

### **2. Improve the Quality of Learning**
- **Goal:** Ensure that children learn basic literacy and numeracy skills.
- **Key Fact:**
  - **250 million children** cannot read, write, or count well—even after four years of schooling.

- **Actions Required:**
  - Recruit **2 million more well-trained teachers** with effective performance measures.
  - Provide improved traditional and digital learning materials in students’ home languages.
  - Increase early-childhood education programs by **45%** to prepare children for primary school.
  - Align schooling with workforce needs and promote adult learning.

---

### **3. Foster Global Citizenship**
- **Goal:** Prepare students to be responsible global citizens.
- **Key Focus Areas:**
  - Promote peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity across cultures.
  - Cultivate community, active participation, and environmental stewardship.
  - Train teachers in innovative, participatory, and non-formal teaching methods.

- **Quote from Ban Ki-moon:**
  > “Education gives us a profound understanding that we are tied together as citizens of the global community, and that our challenges are interconnected.”

---

### **Close the Financial Gap**
To achieve these goals, significant investment is needed:
- **Increase global investment** in poor countries' education systems by **$24 billion per year**.
- Invest at least **5 to 6% of national GDP** in education.
- Ensure **effective and accountable use** of resources aligned with national education plans.

> **Ban Ki-moon Quote:**  
> “Education is the single best investment countries can make towards building prosperous, healthy and equitable societies.”

---

### **Overall Vision**
Ban Ki-moon emphasizes that **education reduces poverty and hunger**, **ends wasted potential**, and leads to **stronger, better societies**. He believes that when education is prioritized, it empowers individuals and nations alike.
