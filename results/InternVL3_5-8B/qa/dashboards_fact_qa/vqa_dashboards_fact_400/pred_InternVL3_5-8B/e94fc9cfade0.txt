The three basic approaches for organizing a network, as outlined in the image, are:

1. **Crawling**: Search engines follow links to browse the 60+ billion (and growing) pages that make up the web.
2. **Sorting**: Results get sorted by a variety of factors (more on this in a second).
3. **Indexing**: Sorted pages get stored in an index so search engines can match queries with results faster.

These steps are fundamental to how search engines like Google organize and retrieve information from the vast expanse of the internet.
