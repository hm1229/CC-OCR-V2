To calculate the **percentage of paid apps on the iPhone and Android stores taken together**, we need to:

1. Extract the **paid app percentages** for each store from the infographic.
2. Combine them using a **weighted average**, based on the number of available apps in each store (since they have different total app counts).

---

### Step 1: Get Paid App Percentages

From the “Heavy Hitters” section:

- **Apple App Store for iPhone**:  
  → 71% paid apps (29% free)

- **Google Android Market**:  
  → 41% paid apps (60% free — note: this adds to 101%, likely rounding; we’ll use 41% as given)

*(Note: The Blackberry data is not needed since the question only asks for iPhone + Android.)*

---

### Step 2: Get Total Available Apps per Store

From “Top Mobile App Stores”:

- **iPhone**: 375,566 apps  
- **Android**: 224,808 apps  

Total apps (iPhone + Android) = 375,566 + 224,808 = **600,374**

---

### Step 3: Calculate Weighted Average Paid App Percentage

We compute:

> **(Paid %_iPhone × Apps_iPhone + Paid %_Android × Apps_Android) / Total Apps**

= (0.71 × 375,566 + 0.41 × 224,808) / 600,374

Calculate numerator:

- 0.71 × 375,566 = **266,651.86**
- 0.41 × 224,808 = **92,171.28**
- Sum = 266,651.86 + 92,171.28 = **358,823.14**

Now divide by total apps:

→ 358,823.14 / 600,374 ≈ **0.5977**

---

### Final Answer:

≈ **59.8%**

---

✅ So, the percentage of paid apps on the iPhone and Android stores, taken together, is approximately **59.8%**.

*(Note: If the question intended a simple unweighted average — i.e., just averaging 71% and 41% — that would be 56%. But since the stores have very different sizes, the weighted average is more accurate and appropriate.)*

---

**Answer: \boxed{59.8\%}**
