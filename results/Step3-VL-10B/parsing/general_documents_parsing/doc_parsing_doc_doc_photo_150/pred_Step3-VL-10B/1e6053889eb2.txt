\section*{Problem 1}
500 people were surveyed and asked to check the boxes that apply to them.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{a.} I am confident in the economy.
    \item \textbf{b.} I have health insurance.
\end{itemize}
321 said they were confident about the economy, 403 said they had health insurance, and 63 checked neither box.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{[3 pts]} Draw an accurate Venn diagram that represents the situation consisting of cardinalities.
    \item \textbf{[1 pt]} How many people had health insurance and were not confident in the economy?
    \item \textbf{[1 pt]} How many people had health insurance or are confident in the economy?
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Problem 2}
Using the digits 0 through 9, find out how many 4-digit numbers can be configured based on the stated conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{[1 pt]} The number cannot begin with zero and no digits can be repeated.
    \item \textbf{[1 pt]} The number must begin and end with an odd digit. (Repeated digits are okay.)
    \item \textbf{[1 pt]} The number must be at least 6000. (Repeated digits are okay.)
    \item \textbf{[2 pts]} The number must be less than 3000 and can be even. No digits may be repeated in the last 3 digits, but 2234 would not be okay. (That is, 2234 would be okay, but 2334 would not be okay.)
\end{enumerate}
