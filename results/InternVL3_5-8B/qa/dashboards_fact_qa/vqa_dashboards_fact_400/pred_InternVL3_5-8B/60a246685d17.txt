The antibody test that involves finger-prick testing is the home test. This type of test is designed to be conducted at home, where a small amount of blood is obtained through a finger-prick. The blood sample is then used to detect the presence of antibodies against the virus. This method is less invasive and more convenient for individuals who prefer to test themselves in the comfort of their own homes. However, it is important to note that these tests are currently not accurate enough for widespread use and are only available for specific groups such as NHS and social care staff, and NHS and care home patients based on their clinician's advice.
