During the **Battle of Blore Heath** in 1459, the **Lancastrians** had greater numerical strength compared to the Yorkists.

- **Lancastrians**: 8,000–14,000 men  
- **Yorkists**: 3,000–5,000 men  

Thus, the Lancastrians significantly outnumbered the Yorkists. However, despite their numerical advantage, the Yorkists initially held a strong defensive position and managed to inflict heavy casualties before being overwhelmed. The battle ultimately ended in a **Lancastrian victory**, though it was costly and did not decisively end the conflict.
