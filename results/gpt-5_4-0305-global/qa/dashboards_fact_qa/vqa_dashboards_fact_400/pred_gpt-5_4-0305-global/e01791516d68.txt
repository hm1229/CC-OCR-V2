The main phases of matter in chemistry are:

- **Solid** — fixed shape and fixed volume  
- **Liquid** — fixed volume, takes the shape of its container  
- **Gas** — no fixed shape or volume  
- **Plasma** — ionized gas, found in stars, lightning, etc.

Other phases sometimes discussed include:

- **Bose–Einstein condensate** — forms at extremely low temperatures  
- **Liquid crystal** — has properties between liquids and solids

In most basic chemistry, the first four are the standard phases.
