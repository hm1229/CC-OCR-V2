### Context & Meaning:

This slogan is likely part of a campaign related to **MDG 8 (Millennium Development Goal 8)** — *A Global Partnership for Development* — which aimed to foster international cooperation, aid, debt relief, and fair trade to support development in poorer nations.

“**More Than Mines**” could be interpreted in several ways, depending on context:

1. **Anti-landmine advocacy**: Many African countries have suffered from landmines left over from conflicts. The phrase may suggest that children deserve more than dangerous, war-torn environments — they deserve safety, education, and opportunity.

2. **Beyond extractive industries**: In resource-rich but poor countries, “mines” can symbolize exploitation of natural resources without benefiting local populations. “More than mines” could mean communities should benefit from their own resources — through education, infrastructure, health — not just extraction.

3. **Children as more than victims or statistics**: It may also imply that these children are not defined by poverty, conflict, or aid dependency — they are individuals with potential, dreams, and rights.

Given the UN branding (“Copyright © United Nations | UN Photo/JC McIlwaine”) and the MDG 8 theme, this is almost certainly a **UN-led awareness campaign** using powerful imagery and slogans to promote global solidarity and action toward sustainable development.
