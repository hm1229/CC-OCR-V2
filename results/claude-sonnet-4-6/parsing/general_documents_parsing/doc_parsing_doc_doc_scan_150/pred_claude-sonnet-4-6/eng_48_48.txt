\begin{enumerate}
  \item Which is a signal word for present continuous?\\

  \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present continuous?\\
        now - already - still
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.5em}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=pinkbg, linecolor=darkred]
\begin{tabular}{>{\bfseries}p{3cm} p{9cm}}
Form & have or has + Past Participle \\[0.5em]
Use & For actions which started in the past and continued up to the present. \\[0.5em]
Signal words & just, already, yet, for, since, so far, recently, ever\ldots \\
\end{tabular}
\end{mdframed}

\vspace{0.5em}

\noindent\small
NB. We use Present Perfect Continuous (have/has+ been + verb +ing) to give more emphasis on the duration of the action. However, some verbs can't be used in the continuous form such as own, smell, need, belong, cost, agree, contain\ldots, etc.

\vspace{1em}

  \item Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present perfect?\\
        since -- before -- for
\end{enumerate}
