Actually, the area **between the wickets** in cricket is called the **pitch** — more specifically, the **cricket pitch**.

### Key Details:
- The **pitch** is a rectangular strip of ground 22 yards (20.12 meters) long and 10 feet (3.05 meters) wide.
- It lies between the two sets of **wickets** (each consisting of three stumps and two bails).
- This is where most of the action happens: bowlers deliver the ball from one end, batsmen stand at the other, and fielders are positioned around it.
- In the infographic you shared, the central green strip with the wickets at each end is visually representing this **pitch**.

> ⚠️ Note: Sometimes people confuse “pitch” with the entire playing field (the “outfield”), but technically, the **pitch** refers *only* to the central strip between the wickets.
