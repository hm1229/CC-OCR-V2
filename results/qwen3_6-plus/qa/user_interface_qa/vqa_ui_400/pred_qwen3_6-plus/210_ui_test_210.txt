The infographic mentions **three** basic approaches that all modern search engines use to organize websites:

1. **Crawling** – Search engines follow links to browse the web’s pages.
2. **Sorting** – Results are sorted by a variety of factors (e.g., relevance, keywords, location).
3. **Indexing** – Sorted pages are stored in an index for faster query matching.

These three steps — Crawling, Sorting, and Indexing — are clearly labeled with icons and descriptions under the section “SEARCH ENGINE BASICS.”

✅ So, the answer is: **3 approaches**

*(Note: While algorithms differ between engines like Google, Bing, and Yahoo, the foundational organizational approach — crawl → sort → index — is presented as universal across all modern search engines.)*
