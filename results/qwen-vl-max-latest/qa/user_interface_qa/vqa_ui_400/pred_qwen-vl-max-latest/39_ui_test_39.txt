The different phases of matter in chemistry, as shown in the image "The Map of Chemistry," are:

1. **Solid** – A state where particles are tightly packed and have a fixed shape and volume.
2. **Liquid** – A state where particles are close together but can move past one another, giving it a definite volume but no fixed shape.
3. **Gas** – A state where particles are far apart and move freely, having neither a fixed shape nor volume.
4. **Plasma** – A high-energy state where atoms are ionized, forming a mixture of free electrons and ions (often found in stars or lightning).

These phases are illustrated in the central section of the map under the heading "PHASES." The image also shows transitions between these states, such as melting, freezing, evaporation, condensation, sublimation, and deposition.
