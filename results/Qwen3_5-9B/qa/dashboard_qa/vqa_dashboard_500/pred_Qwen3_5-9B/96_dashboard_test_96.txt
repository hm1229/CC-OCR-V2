Based on the infographic, **82%** of women over the age of 50 have an incidence of cancer.

Here’s how we calculate that:

The infographic states:
> “Only 18% of cancers in women occur before the age of 50”

This means that the remaining percentage — those occurring **at or after age 50** — is:

**100% - 18% = 82%**

Additionally, the “Incidence by Age” donut chart breaks down the distribution among older age groups:
- 50 to 64 yrs: 27%
- 65 to 74 yrs: 25%
- Over 74 yrs: 30%

Adding these:  
**27% + 25% + 30% = 82%**

✅ So, **82% of cancer cases in women occur in those aged 50 and older**.

*(Note: The infographic says “Cancer is more likely in older women” and explicitly states only 18% occur before age 50 — so the 82% figure for age 50+ is consistent with that.)*
