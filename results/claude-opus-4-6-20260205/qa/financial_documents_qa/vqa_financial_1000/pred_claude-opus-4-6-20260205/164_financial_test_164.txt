According to the article from **The Denver Post** dated **Friday, July 16, 1976**, Yugoslavia pardoned **Lazlo Toth**, a **Thornton resident** who had been arrested almost a year earlier for **"industrial espionage."** 

Toth, age 46, was a naturalized American citizen who was vacationing in Yugoslavia when he was detained on August 1 and officially arrested about two weeks later. He was the Director of Research and Development at **Great Western Sugar Co.** in Loveland, Colorado, and had been charged with industrial espionage and sentenced to seven years after a closed-door trial. He was pardoned by the **Yugoslav collective presidency**, the nine-man ruling body that included **President Tito**.
