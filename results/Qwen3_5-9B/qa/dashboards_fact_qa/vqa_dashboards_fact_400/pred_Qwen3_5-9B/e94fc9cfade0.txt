Based on the infographic “The Science Behind Google and Other Search Engines,” the three basic approaches for organizing websites (which form the foundation of how search engines operate) are:

1. **Crawling**  
   Search engines follow links to browse the 60 trillion (and growing) pages that make up the web.

2. **Sorting**  
   Results get sorted by a variety of factors — more on this in a second!

3. **Indexing**  
   Sorted pages get stored in an index so search engines can match queries with results faster.

These three steps — Crawling, Sorting, and Indexing — are used by all modern search engines to organize the web.

*(Note: The infographic uses “network” loosely to refer to the web or collection of websites — not computer networks in the technical sense.)*
