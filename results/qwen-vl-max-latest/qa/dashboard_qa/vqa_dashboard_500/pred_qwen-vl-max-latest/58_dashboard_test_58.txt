Australia has **six states** and **two territories**, making a total of **eight** administrative divisions. The six states are:

1. New South Wales (NSW)  
2. Victoria (VIC)  
3. Queensland (QLD)  
4. Western Australia (WA)  
5. South Australia (SA)  
6. Tasmania (TAS)  

The two territories are:

- Northern Territory (NT)  
- Australian Capital Territory (ACT)  

In the image, all eight are listed at the bottom: NSW, VIC, QLD, WA, SA, NT, TAS, and ACT. So while there are **six states**, the total number of jurisdictions shown is **eight**.
