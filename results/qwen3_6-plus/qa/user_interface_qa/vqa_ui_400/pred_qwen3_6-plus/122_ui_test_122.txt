The **Louisiana Purchase** is regarded as one of the greatest real estate deals in U.S. history.

In 1803, President Thomas Jefferson led the acquisition of approximately **828,000 square miles** of land from France — stretching from the Mississippi River to the Rocky Mountains and from the Gulf of Mexico to the Canadian border — for just **$15 million**. This doubled the size of the United States overnight and included territory that now makes up all or part of **15 different states**.

The infographic emphasizes its significance by noting:
- It was a strategic move to secure the Port of New Orleans and control of the Mississippi River.
- The $15 million price tag would be about **$284 million today** — still an incredible bargain.
- In 2010 alone, goods and services imported and exported through Louisiana ports (including New Orleans) totaled **$100 billion**, highlighting the long-term economic value.

So, while it’s called the “Louisiana Purchase,” it wasn’t just about Louisiana — it was a massive, transformative land deal that reshaped the nation.

✅ **Answer: The Louisiana Purchase**
