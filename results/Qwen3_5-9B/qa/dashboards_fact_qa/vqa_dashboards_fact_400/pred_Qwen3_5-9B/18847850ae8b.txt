Based on the provided infographic titled **“Best Job Search Techniques of 2013”**, there is **no single “best” method** declared — instead, it presents a variety of techniques and how people use them, along with their perceived effectiveness or popularity.

Here’s a breakdown of key insights from the infographic:

---

### 📊 **Ways We Search for Jobs (2013)**
- **Online recruitment platforms**: 26%
- **Personal research & contacts**: 11%
- **Recruitment agency**: 9%
- **Traditional media** (newspapers, etc.): 7%
- **Social media**: 6%
- **41% say they use ALL these methods!**

> ✅ This suggests that **using multiple methods together** is common and likely more effective than relying on just one.

---

### 💼 **How We Prepare for Interviews**
- Research the company: **52.4%**
- Relax to ease nerves: **33.9%**
- Practice interview questions: **10.5%**
- Videotape themselves: **3.2%**

> 🎯 **Researching the company** is the most common preparation tactic — implying it’s seen as valuable.

---

### 🔍 **The “Hidden Job Market”**
- **41%** have never tried applying for a role that wasn’t advertised.
- **36%** reckon existing employees benefit most from these vacancies.

> 👥 This highlights that **networking and internal referrals** (part of “personal research & contacts”) may be underutilized but potentially high-yield.

---

### 💻 **How Job Seekers View Technology**
- **57%** want search techniques to continue evolving.
- **41%** say there are now better opportunities accessible via tech.
- **21%** still prefer traditional job search methods.

> 🌐 Technology is widely embraced, though some still favor old-school approaches.

---

### 😞 **We Still Blame the Economy**
- **27.6%** blame economic difficulties for forcing extreme measures.
- **11.3%** say desperation kicks in and forces new techniques.

> ⚠️ Economic pressure drives innovation in job search — but not necessarily success.

---

### 🚀 **Inventive Techniques Already Tested**
- Made a video CV: **8.7%**
- Put CV on a T-shirt: **7%**
- Would put CV on a Picket Board: **13.3%**
- Would advertise need for job on Billboard: **8.1%**

> 🤡 These are creative but niche — not mainstream “best” practices.

---

## ✅ So, What’s the “Best” Method?

According to this 2013 data:

> **There is no single best method — the most effective approach appears to be using a combination of methods**, especially:
> - **Online recruitment platforms** (most popular at 26%)
> - **Personal research & contacts** (highly effective due to the “hidden job market”)
> - **Interview preparation** (especially researching the company)
> - **Leveraging technology** (57% want it to evolve; 41% see it as improving access)

Also note: **41% of job seekers use ALL listed methods** — suggesting **diversification = higher success rate**.

---

## 🏆 Final Answer:
> **The “best” job search technique in 2013 was using multiple methods together — particularly online platforms, personal networks, and thorough interview prep — rather than relying on any single approach.**
