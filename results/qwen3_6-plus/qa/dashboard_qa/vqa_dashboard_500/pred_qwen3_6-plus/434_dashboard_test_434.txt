The first feature-length film released with synchronized sound was **The Jazz Singer (1927)**.

As shown in the infographic, it is credited as the “First feature-length film with synchronized sound,” marking a pivotal moment in cinema history by transitioning from silent films to “talkies.”

While earlier experiments and short films had used synchronized sound, *The Jazz Singer* was the first full-length motion picture to successfully integrate synchronized dialogue and musical numbers using the Vitaphone sound-on-disc system, making it a landmark achievement in film technology and storytelling.

---

**Note**: Although *Fantasia (1940)* is listed for its theatrical implementation of 3D multi-channel sound (Fantasound), that came much later and was not the *first* synchronized sound film — just an advanced evolution of it.

✅ So, the correct answer is: **The Jazz Singer (1927)**.
