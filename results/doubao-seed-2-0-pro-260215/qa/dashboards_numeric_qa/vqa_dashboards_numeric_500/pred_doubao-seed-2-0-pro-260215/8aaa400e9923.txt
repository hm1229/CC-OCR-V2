The developmental benefits of early childhood education fall into 3 categories:
1.  **Social and emotional**
    - Children are surrounded by adults and peers, and learn to develop relationships.
    - The constant presence of these people provides a sense of security.
    - This sense of security leads to greater participation and cooperation in activities/learning.
2.  **Cognitive**
    - The developmental curriculum provides age-appropriate learning suited to the child's stage.
    - Children build on their existing skills, growing their knowledge base.
    - Teachers support children to reach important developmental milestones.
3.  **Language**
    - Language develops very rapidly between ages 1 and 5, and regular interactions with teachers and peers help build language skills in this window.
    - More advanced language skills develop as conversations and vocabulary grow with these regular interactions.
