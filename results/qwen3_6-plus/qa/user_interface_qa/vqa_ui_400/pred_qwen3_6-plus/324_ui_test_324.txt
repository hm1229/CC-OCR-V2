According to the infographic **“Reading Like a Sleuth”**, under the section:

> **WHAT CLOSE READERS DO DIFFERENTLY**

It contrasts “Close Readers” with “Not-So-Close Readers.”

Under the column **“NOT-SO-CLOSE READERS...”**, one of the listed behaviors is:

> **Ignore syntax clues.**

---

✅ So, the answer is:  
**Not-so-close readers** ignore syntax clues.

This is presented as a key difference — close readers *pay attention to language* (which includes syntax), while not-so-close readers overlook these structural and grammatical hints that help uncover deeper meaning.
