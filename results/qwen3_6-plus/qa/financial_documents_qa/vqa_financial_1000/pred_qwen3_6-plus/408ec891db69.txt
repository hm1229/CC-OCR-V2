Based on the provided document, specifically under section **ix) Foreign currency transactions**, the following are taken to the statement of profit and loss on settlement of transactions:

> **Gains/losses on settlement of the transactions** are taken to the statement of profit and loss.

Additionally, the text clarifies that:

- Transactions in foreign currency are recorded at the exchange rates prevailing at the time of transaction.
- Monetary items are translated at year-end rates, and any resulting gains/losses from that translation are also taken to the statement of profit and loss.
- For forward contracts, the difference between the forward rate and the spot rate at inception is recognized over the life of the contract as income or expense. Also, the exchange difference between the reporting/settlement date rate and the inception/last reporting date rate is recognized as income or expense for the period — which ultimately flows into the statement of profit and loss.

---

✅ So, to directly answer your question:

> **Gains or losses arising on settlement of foreign currency transactions** are taken to the statement of profit and loss.

This includes both realized gains/losses from settling transactions and unrealized gains/losses from revaluing monetary items at year-end rates.
