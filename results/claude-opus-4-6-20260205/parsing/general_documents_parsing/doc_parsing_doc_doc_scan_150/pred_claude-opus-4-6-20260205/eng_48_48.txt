\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}
\node[circle, fill=darkred, text=white, font=\Large\bfseries, minimum size=1cm] {5};
\node[right, font=\Large\bfseries] at (0.7,0) {PRESENT PERFECT};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\begin{tabular}{|l|p{8cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Form} & have or has + Past Participle \\
\hline
\textbf{Use} & For actions which started in the past and continued up to the present. \\
\hline
\textbf{Signal words} & just, already, yet, for, since, so far, recently, ever... \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent NB. We use Present Perfect Continuous (have/has+ been + verb +ing) to give more emphasis on the duration of the action. However, some verbs can't be used in the continuous form such as own, smell, need, belong, cost, agree, contain..., etc.

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent\fcolorbox{darkred}{darkred}{\color{white}\textbf{EXERCISE}} \tikz\node[circle, fill=darkred, text=white, inner sep=2pt, font=\bfseries]{1}; \textbf{Find the signal words for present perfect.}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent 1. Which is a signal word for present perfect?\\
\hspace*{0.5cm} still -- usually -- since\\
2. Which is \textbf{not} a signal word for present perfect?\\
\hspace*{0.5cm} since -- before -- for
