Based on the infographic, **dry dog food** has a longer shelf life than canned dog food.

Here’s the breakdown from the “BEST IF USED BY: STORING DOG FOOD” section:

### Canned Dog Food:
- **Shelf life (unopened):** 18–24 months from date packaged.
- **Once opened:** Store at <40°F and keeps for **3 days** (refrigerated).

### Dry Dog Food:
- **Shelf life (unopened):** 12–15 months from date packaged.
- **Once opened:** Store at <80°F and keeps for **90 days** (in a cool, dry place).

---

✅ **Conclusion:**
While **canned food lasts longer unopened** (up to 24 months vs. 15 months), **dry food lasts much longer once opened** (90 days vs. 3 days). 

However, if you’re comparing **total usable shelf life under typical storage conditions**, **dry dog food generally offers a longer practical shelf life**, especially since it’s often stored in bulk and opened less frequently.

> 🐶 *Note: The infographic says canned food is 18–24 months unopened, while dry is 12–15 — so technically, canned wins on unopened shelf life. But dry food’s 90-day post-opening window vs. canned’s 3-day window makes dry food more forgiving for long-term storage after opening.*

So, depending on your definition of “shelf life”:
- **Unopened?** → **Canned food** (longer)
- **Opened & stored properly?** → **Dry food** (much longer)

But since most people store food unopened until use, and then open it once, **canned food technically has a longer initial shelf life**.
