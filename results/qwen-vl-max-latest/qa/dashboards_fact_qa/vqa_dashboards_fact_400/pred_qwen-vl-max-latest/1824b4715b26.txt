To achieve an equal standard of living, a Washington D.C. resident needs to earn **$13,000 more** than a Kentucky resident.

This is calculated as follows:

- After-tax earnings needed in D.C.: **$53,000**
- After-tax earnings in Kentucky: **$40,000**
- Difference: $53,000 - $40,000 = **$13,000**

So, a DC resident must earn **$13,000 more** than a KY resident to match the standard of living due to differences in taxes and cost of goods.
