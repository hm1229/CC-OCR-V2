According to the infographic **“Reading Like a Sleuth”**, under the section:

> **10 QUESTIONS ALL CLOSE READERS ASK**

One of the questions listed is:

> **“What happens in the text?”**

This question is asked by **close readers** — students or readers who are practicing close reading skills.

The infographic positions this as one of the key analytical questions that close readers use to dig deeper into a text, alongside others like:
- “What is the text about?”
- “Who is speaking in the text?”
- “What words do I notice?”
- “Why did the author write the text?”

These questions help readers move beyond surface-level understanding and engage critically with the material — which is the whole point of “reading like a sleuth.”

---

✅ So, to directly answer your question:  
> **Close readers** ask *“What happens in the text?”*

This is part of their strategy to uncover deeper meaning, analyze structure, and support interpretations with textual evidence — as outlined in the CCSS (Common Core State Standards) section of the infographic.
