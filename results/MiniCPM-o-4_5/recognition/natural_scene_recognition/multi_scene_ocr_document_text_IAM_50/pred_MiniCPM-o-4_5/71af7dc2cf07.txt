Strangely enough, the Berlin audience received the film with extreme coolness, much preferring Jean-luc Godard's disappointing "Une Femme est une Femme," a ninety-one minute hymn to "rogue," "Cahiers du Cinema," and the worst aspects of the American cinema. From a brilliantly funny start, the work fizzles out into a series of repetitious sight-gags and personal jokes incomprehensible to the uninitiated (including four plugs for Charles Aznavour).
